# Purpose

The Intelligence Spectrum defines how intelligence capability is represented across all forms of existence within Project Draugr.

The spectrum does not classify species.

It does not determine superiority.

It does not define worth.

It defines:

- awareness capability;
- complexity of thought;
- ability to adapt;
- ability to influence reality.

---

# Core Principle

Intelligence exists as a continuum.

There are no absolute boundaries between levels.

An entity may:

- develop gradually;
- evolve suddenly;
- regress;
- transform;
- transcend traditional classifications.

The spectrum exists as a simulation framework, not as a limitation.

---

# Intelligence Measurement Model

An entity's intelligence is determined by multiple dimensions:

```
Intelligence State

=

Awareness

+

Memory

+

Learning

+

Decision Complexity

+

Adaptation

+

Influence
```

A single numerical value is insufficient.

Two entities may have the same intelligence level but completely different intelligence expressions.

---

# Intelligence Dimensions

## Awareness

The ability to perceive and understand existence.

Measures:

- self recognition;
- environmental understanding;
- social understanding;
- existential understanding.

---

## Memory

The ability to retain information across time.

Measures:

- experience retention;
- knowledge accumulation;
- historical continuity.

---

## Learning

The ability to change behavior based on experience.

Measures:

- adaptation speed;
- discovery;
- improvement.

---

## Decision Complexity

The ability to evaluate possible outcomes.

Measures:

- reaction;
- instinct;
- reasoning;
- strategy;
- long-term planning.

---

## Adaptation

The ability to transform according to conditions.

Measures:

- survival;
- evolution;
- innovation.

---

## Influence

The ability to affect surrounding reality.

Measures:

- individual impact;
- social impact;
- universal impact.

# Intelligence Levels

The following levels represent increasing complexity.

They are not species classifications.

# The Overseer Boundary

The Overseer does not belong to the Intelligence Spectrum.

The relationship is:

```
                 THE OVERSEER

                       |

              Intelligence Spectrum

                       |

        Level 12 Absolute Intelligence
```

The Overseer is not:

- Level 13;
- higher intelligence;
- ultimate intelligence.

The Overseer exists outside the measurement system.

# Intelligence Regression

Intelligence may also decrease.

Possible causes:

- memory loss;
- destruction;
- corruption;
- collapse;
- environmental change.

A civilization may fall from:

```
Level 7

↓

Level 4

↓

Level 3
```

if knowledge and structure are lost.

# Intelligence and Balance

Higher intelligence creates greater responsibility.

Greater influence increases the impact on:

- creation;
- destruction;
- universal balance.

An entity's intelligence determines potential influence.

Its choices determine consequences.

---

# Final Principle

The Intelligence Spectrum does not define what an entity is.

It defines what an entity is capable of understanding, becoming, and influencing.

The universe does not contain fixed categories.

It contains:

```
Existence

↓

Awareness

↓

Intelligence

↓

Evolution

↓

Unknown Possibility
```

Every entity has a beginning.

No entity has a predetermined ending.
