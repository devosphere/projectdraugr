# Purpose

The Existence Law and Governance System defines how intelligent entities establish rules, authority structures, justice systems, and collective decision-making mechanisms to maintain stability and coexistence.

This system governs:

- laws;
- governance;
- authority;
- justice;
- rights;
- responsibilities;
- political structures;
- civilization administration;
- inter-civilization agreements;
- universal governance principles.

# Governance Models

---

# Individual Authority

One entity controls decisions.

Advantages:

- speed;
- simplicity.

Risks:

- corruption;
- dependency.

---

# Hierarchical Governance

Authority distributed through levels.

Advantages:

- organization;
- specialization.

Risks:

- inequality;
- bureaucracy.

---

# Collective Governance

Decisions shared among many entities.

Advantages:

- representation;
- diversity.

Risks:

- slower decisions.

---

# Distributed Governance

Authority spread across independent systems.

Advantages:

- resilience;
- adaptability.

Risks:

- coordination difficulty.
