# Purpose

The Goal and Purpose System defines why entities act, what they seek, and how their objectives develop throughout existence.

This system governs:

- individual motivations;
- survival instincts;
- personal ambitions;
- relationships;
- civilizations;
- species objectives;
- higher existence purposes;
- universal-scale intentions.

The system ensures that entities are not simply reacting to events.

They are pursuing meaningful directions.

---

# Core Principle

> **Every entity moves toward something, even if that purpose is unknown, unconscious, or beyond its understanding.**

Purpose may exist as:

- instinct;
- desire;
- necessity;
- curiosity;
- belief;
- responsibility;
- existential principle.

# Purpose vs Goal

Goals and purpose are connected but different.

---

# Goal

A specific objective an entity attempts to achieve.

Examples:

- find food;
- build a house;
- defeat an enemy;
- discover knowledge.

---

# Purpose

The deeper reason behind actions.

Examples:

Goal:

```
Build a kingdom
```

Purpose:

```
Create security and legacy
```

---

# Entity Goal Profile (EGP)

Every entity possesses a Goal Profile.

```
Entity Goal Profile

├── Goal Conflicts

# Goal Categories

Goals exist at different scales.

---

# 1. Survival Goals

The most fundamental goals.

Purpose:

```
Continue Existence
```

Examples:

- obtain resources;
- avoid danger;
- maintain health;
- reproduce.

---

# Survival Instinct

Lower intelligence entities may have only survival goals.

Example:

```
Threat

↓

Avoidance

↓

Survival
```

---

# 2. Growth Goals

Goals related to improvement.

Examples:

- gain knowledge;
- develop skills;
- become stronger;
- evolve.

---

# 3. Social Goals

Goals involving other entities.

Examples:

- friendship;
- family;
- loyalty;
- cooperation;
- leadership.

---

# 4. Achievement Goals

Goals related to accomplishment.

Examples:

- discovery;
- creation;
- exploration;
- mastery.

---

# 5. Legacy Goals

Goals related to future impact.

Examples:

- creating civilization;
- preserving knowledge;
- becoming remembered.

---

# 6. Existential Goals

Goals connected to the nature of existence.

Examples:

- maintaining balance;
- protecting reality;
- understanding existence.

```

- stagnation;
