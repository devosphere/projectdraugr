# 18.6 – Construction UI

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *Construction changes the world. Crafting changes the object.*

---

# Purpose

The Construction UI allows players to build permanent or semi-permanent structures within the world.

Unlike Crafting, which produces portable objects, Construction creates structures that occupy physical space and become part of the world's geography.

Construction is one of the primary ways players permanently shape the world.

---

# Design Philosophy

Project Draugr follows one immutable principle:

> **Structures are built because they physically exist—not because the game provides building pieces.**

Construction is an engineering activity.

It requires:

- knowledge
- materials
- time
- labor
- suitable terrain
- sufficient space

The Construction UI never behaves like a creative-mode building editor.

---

# Construction vs Crafting

Crafting produces portable objects.

Examples:

- Stone Axe
- Rope
- Sling Carrier
- Wooden Bowl

Construction produces world structures.

Examples:

- Shelter
- Fence
- Bridge
- Storage Hut
- Workshop
- Watch Tower

Crafted objects may become construction materials.

Example:

```text id="f0wz2q"
Craft

↓

Wooden Planks

↓

Construct

↓

Wooden Floor
```

---

# Opening the Construction UI

The Construction UI appears only when intentionally requested.

Examples:

```text id="jajm2j"
Open construction.

Build.

Show construction.
```

The UI never opens automatically.

---

# Knowledge-Driven Construction

Available construction projects depend entirely upon the Chronicle's knowledge.

The UI only displays structures the Chronicle understands how to build.

Example:

New survivor:

```text id="tqivgw"
Available

• Campfire
• Lean-to Shelter
```

Experienced builder:

```text id="hgng5w"
Available

• Campfire
• Lean-to Shelter
• Fence
• Storage Rack
• Work Table
• Drying Rack
```

Master builder:

```text id="cqjlwm"
Available

• Cabin
• Workshop
• Watch Tower
• Wooden Bridge
• Defensive Wall
• Warehouse
```

Unknown structures remain hidden.

# Site Requirements

Construction requires an appropriate location.

Examples:

Bridge

Requires:

- river crossing
- suitable span
- stable banks

Shelter

Requires:

- enough ground space
- reasonably level terrain

Watch Tower

Requires:

- stable foundation
- vertical clearance

Construction cannot begin if the environment is unsuitable.

---

# Materials

Structures consume real materials.

Missing materials are displayed clearly.

Example:

```text id="2g05ux"
Materials

✓ 12 × Logs

✓ 20 × Plant Fiber

✗ Large Stones
```

Construction never generates missing resources.

---

# Tools

Some structures require tools.

Examples:

- Stone Axe
- Hammer
- Shovel
- Chisel

Without the required tools, construction cannot begin.

---

# Time

Construction consumes real time.

Example:

```text id="vjlwm3"
Estimated Time

4 Hours
```

Time advances naturally while construction is performed.

The world continues evolving.

---

# Work Progress

Large structures are not completed instantly.

Construction may occur over multiple sessions.

Example:

```text id="egxt8t"
Watch Tower

Progress

41%
```

Players may stop and resume later.

Incomplete structures remain in the world.

---

# Construction Failure

Poor conditions may affect construction quality.

Examples:

- insufficient tools
- poor materials
- physical exhaustion
- bad weather

Results may include:

- unstable structures
- slower completion
- reduced durability
- structural collapse during construction

---

# Infrastructure

Many constructions become Infrastructure.

Examples:

- Work Tables
- Workshops
- Kilns
- Wells
- Roads

Infrastructure improves the environment rather than directly improving the Chronicle.

Refer to:

**15 – Infrastructure**

---

# Physical Persistence

Every constructed structure exists physically.

Examples:

- Houses
- Workshops
- Roads
- Bridges
- Walls

They remain after:

- player logout
- Chronicle death
- abandonment

Until destroyed, they permanently become part of the world.

---

# Ownership

Structures have no magical ownership.

Any accessible structure may later be:

- used
- repaired
- modified
- destroyed
- occupied
- stolen

Future security systems may restrict access through:

- locks
- gates
- guards
- traps

Ownership is social—not mechanical.

---

# Legacy

Construction is one of the strongest forms of legacy.

Future Chronicles may discover:

- abandoned settlements
- ruined workshops
- collapsed bridges
- forgotten roads
- defensive walls

Civilizations become visible through their constructions.

---

# Future Expansion

The Construction UI may later support:

- collaborative building
- structural maintenance
- repairs
- upgrades
- modular construction
- engineering analysis
- blueprint planning
- settlement planning

These additions must preserve the core philosophy.

---

# Final Principle

Construction does not place game objects.

Construction changes the world itself.

Every structure occupies space.

Every structure tells a story.

Long after its builders are gone, the world remembers what they built.
