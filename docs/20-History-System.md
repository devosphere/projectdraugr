# 20 – History System

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *The world never forgets. It only waits to be rediscovered.*

---

# Purpose

The History System records every persistent event that permanently changes the world.

Unlike traditional games, Project Draugr does not reset the world after a player's death.

Every Chronicle contributes to a continuous history shared by all future Chronicles.

History transforms individual actions into civilization.

---

# Design Philosophy

Project Draugr follows one immutable principle:

> **The world remembers actions—not players.**

The History System exists independently of any Chronicle.

It records what happened, where it happened, and when it happened.

History is not written to entertain.

It emerges naturally from simulation.

---

# Scope

The History System records only events that permanently affect the world.

Examples include:

- settlements founded
- structures constructed
- infrastructure completed
- forests cleared
- bridges built
- caves discovered
- disasters
- extinctions
- abandoned camps
- Chronicle deaths

Temporary events are not recorded unless they produce lasting consequences.

---

# Persistence

History is permanent.

Events are never deleted.

Even if structures collapse or civilizations disappear, the historical record remains.

The world always remembers what once existed.

---

# Chronicle Contributions

Every Chronicle contributes to history through its actions.

Examples:

- building roads
- creating workshops
- founding settlements
- discovering ruins
- introducing new technologies
- documenting geography

The Chronicle may die.

Its contributions remain.

---

# World Changes

History records changes to the environment.

Examples:

Natural

- volcanic eruptions
- floods
- landslides
- forest fires

Human

- roads
- bridges
- mines
- farms
- defensive walls

Both become part of the world's timeline.

---

# Historical Identity

Historical records reference the Chronicle responsible whenever known.

Example

```text id="snm0nr"
Built by

Chronicle CHR-000018
```

If the Chronicle's name was engraved or publicly recorded, that information may also survive.

Otherwise, future generations may only know that an unknown survivor once built it.

---

# Discoverability

History is not automatically known.

Future Chronicles learn history through:

- exploration
- journals
- books
- maps
- inscriptions
- monuments
- oral traditions (future)

A forgotten civilization remains forgotten until rediscovered.

---

# Archaeology

The History System naturally supports archaeology.

Examples:

A ruined bridge implies:

- someone once crossed here.

A collapsed workshop implies:

- people once crafted here.

An abandoned road implies:

- regular travel once existed.

Players reconstruct history through surviving evidence.

---

# Civilization Timeline

As the simulation continues, isolated events become historical eras.

Example

```text id="kltmna"
The First Settlers

↓

The Expansion Era

↓

The Great Fire

↓

The Iron Age

↓

The River Kingdom
```

These eras are never predefined.

They emerge from player activity.

---

# Destruction

History records destruction as faithfully as creation.

Examples

```text id="6dxuoa"
The northern watchtower collapsed after years of neglect.
```

```text id="0mjlwm"
The western settlement was abandoned.
```

Destruction is part of history.

Nothing is protected from time.

---

# Historical Records

The world may preserve history through physical objects.

Examples:

- journals
- engraved stones
- monuments
- books
- maps
- engineering plans
- cemetery markers

Destroying these objects may erase access to history—but not the events themselves.

---

# Relationship with Other Systems

The History System connects naturally with:

Chronicles

Every Chronicle contributes.

Infrastructure

Infrastructure permanently changes history.

Construction

Buildings become historical landmarks.

Knowledge

Books and journals preserve historical understanding.

Geography

Maps evolve alongside history.

---

# Future Expansion

The History System may later support:

- historical timeline viewer
- archaeology mechanics
- regional histories
- dynasties
- civilizations
- historical monuments
- cultural evolution
- historical books generated by AI
- museums
- libraries

These additions must preserve the same philosophy.

---

# Final Principle

History is not scripted.

It is simulated.

Every Chronicle lives only once.

But the consequences of that life become part of the world forever.

Future generations will not remember every survivor.

They will remember the world those survivors left behind.
