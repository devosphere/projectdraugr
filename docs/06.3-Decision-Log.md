# 06.3 – Decision Log

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *Every important decision should be remembered.*

---

# Purpose

This document records major architectural, gameplay, technical, and creative decisions made throughout the development of Project Draugr.

Its purpose is to preserve the reasoning behind important decisions so future contributors understand **why** a decision was made—not merely **what** was decided.

This document serves as the project's institutional memory.

---

# Decision Rules

Every recorded decision should include:

- Decision ID
- Date
- Category
- Decision
- Reasoning
- Impact
- Status

Once accepted, decisions should not be modified.

If a decision changes, create a new entry that supersedes the previous one.

History should never be erased.

---

Possible categories include:

- Vision
- World Design
- Gameplay
- Simulation

# Decision History

---

## DR-0001

**Date**

2026-07

**Category**

Vision

**Status**

Accepted

### Decision

Project Draugr is fundamentally a **persistent world simulation**, not an AI-first project.

### Reasoning

Early design iterations centered heavily around large-scale artificial intelligence, where nearly every entity would eventually possess its own intelligence.

Although aligned with the long-term vision, this approach made the MVP financially and technically unrealistic.

The project was re-centered around a persistent world simulation.

Artificial intelligence became a future enhancement rather than the project's foundation.

### Impact

- Simplified MVP scope.
- Reduced technical complexity.
- Preserved long-term vision.
- Established "Simulation First" philosophy.

---

## DR-0002

**Date**

2026-07

**Category**

World Design

**Status**

Accepted

### Decision

The world contains **no living human civilization**.

### Reasoning

Traditional RPGs rely on NPC towns and quest hubs.

Project Draugr instead allows civilization to emerge entirely from player actions.

This strengthens persistence and historical continuity.

### Impact

- Every settlement becomes player-created.
- Exploration emphasizes wilderness.
- Civilization becomes emergent gameplay.

---

## DR-0003

**Date**

2026-07

**Category**

Gameplay

**Status**

Accepted

### Decision

Players do not choose classes or professions.

### Reasoning

Identity should emerge naturally through repeated actions.

The project favors capability developed through practice over predefined character archetypes.

### Impact

- Removed traditional class systems.
- Reinforced knowledge-based progression.
- Increased player freedom.

---

## DR-0004

**Date**

2026-07

**Category**

Simulation

**Status**

Accepted

### Decision

The world operates as a **state-driven simulation**.

### Reasoning

The simulation continuously evaluates the world's current state rather than generating events around player presence.

The player participates within an already living world.

### Impact

- World systems operate independently.
- Stronger immersion.
- Emergent gameplay replaces scripted encounters.

---

## DR-0005

**Date**

2026-07

**Category**

Lore

**Status**

Accepted

### Decision

Replace multiple primordial entities with two primordial principles:

- Creation
- Destruction

### Reasoning

Earlier concepts included multiple domain-specific primordial beings.

The design was simplified into two universal principles that naturally produce existence through equilibrium.

This creates a stronger metaphysical foundation while avoiding unnecessary complexity.

### Impact

- Cleaner world mythology.
- Easier systemic integration.
- Stronger philosophical consistency.

---

## DR-0006

**Date**

2026-07

**Category**

Lore

**Status**

Accepted

### Decision

The Overseer never intervenes in the physical world.

### Reasoning

The Overseer exists solely as the eternal observer and preserver of history.

Separating the Overseer from world influence reinforces mystery and prevents it from becoming a traditional "god" character.

### Impact

- Strengthened world philosophy.
- Maintained narrative mystery.
- Clearly separated observation from simulation.

---

## DR-0007

**Date**

2026-07

**Category**

Development

**Status**

Accepted

### Decision

Every development phase must produce a playable product.

### Reasoning

The project should evolve through complete, stable iterations rather than ambitious but unfinished systems.

Each phase adds depth without invalidating previous work.

### Impact

- Reduced scope creep.
- Improved long-term maintainability.
- Better funding flexibility.

# Superseded Decisions

When a decision changes, do not delete the original.

Instead:

- Change its status to **Superseded**.
- Record the new decision.
- Reference the previous Decision ID.

This preserves the project's design history.

---

# Design Philosophy

Changing a decision is not considered failure.

Projects evolve.

The important principle is that every significant change is documented together with the reasoning behind it.

Future contributors should never need to guess why the project was designed the way it is.

---

# Final Principle

Project Draugr preserves not only the world's history, but also its own development history.

Just as every Chronicle leaves a mark upon the world, every important design decision leaves a mark upon the project.

Nothing meaningful should be forgotten.
