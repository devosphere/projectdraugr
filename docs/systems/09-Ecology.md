# 09 – Ecology

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *Life exists for itself.*

---

# Purpose

This document defines the ecological systems of Project Draugr.

Ecology governs the relationships between living organisms and their environment.

It determines how life naturally grows, competes, reproduces, declines, and adapts over time.

The ecological simulation exists independently of player activity.

Players participate within existing ecosystems.

They do not create them.

---

# Design Philosophy

Every living organism exists because it occupies an ecological role.

Nothing exists solely to provide:

- combat
- loot
- food
- quests
- player progression

Every species belongs somewhere.

Every species influences something.

---

# The Ecosystem

Every geographical region contains one or more ecosystems.

Each ecosystem consists of interconnected:

- vegetation
- wildlife
- monsters
- water
- climate
- terrain

Changes to one component naturally affect the others.

---

# Ecological Balance

The world constantly moves toward equilibrium.

Equilibrium does **not** mean perfect balance.

It means continual adaptation.

Population growth.

Population decline.

Migration.

Competition.

Recovery.

These are all natural expressions of a healthy ecosystem.

---

# Food Chains

Every ecosystem contains food chains.

Examples include:

Plants

↓

Herbivores

↓

Predators

↓

Scavengers

↓

Decomposition

Different ecosystems contain different food chains.

There is no universal hierarchy.

---

# Vegetation

Plants form the foundation of most ecosystems.

Examples include:

- trees
- shrubs
- grasses
- vines
- flowers
- moss
- fungi
- aquatic plants

Vegetation responds naturally to:

- climate
- water availability
- sunlight
- seasonal changes
- environmental disturbance

---

# Wildlife

Wildlife exists independently of players.

Animals naturally:

- eat
- drink
- sleep
- migrate
- reproduce
- defend territory
- avoid danger

Their behavior is determined by programmed survival instincts.

---

# Monsters

Monsters are ecological participants.

They are not treated as supernatural game mechanics.

Different monster species occupy different ecological niches.

Some are apex predators.

Some are scavengers.

Some compete with wildlife.

Some coexist.

The simulation treats monsters as living inhabitants rather than scripted enemies.

---

# Population Dynamics

Every species possesses a population.

Population changes naturally through:

- births
- deaths
- predation
- food availability
- disease
- environmental conditions

Populations should fluctuate continuously.

---

# Migration

Species may relocate naturally.

Reasons include:

- seasonal change
- food scarcity
- territorial pressure
- reproduction
- environmental disturbance

Migration creates a world that feels alive rather than static.

---

# Territory

Many species establish territories.

Territories influence:

- hunting
- breeding
- competition
- encounters

Territories evolve over time.

They are not permanently fixed.

---

# Reproduction

Species reproduce according to their biological characteristics.

Reproduction depends upon:

- available food
- environmental conditions
- population health
- breeding seasons

Reproduction prevents ecosystems from remaining static.

---

# Mortality

Death is an ordinary ecological process.

Causes include:

- starvation
- predation
- illness
- old age
- environmental hazards

Death allows ecosystems to renew themselves.

---

# Decomposition

Dead organisms return nutrients to the environment.

Decomposition supports future plant growth.

Nothing disappears without consequence.

Life continually transforms into new life.

---

# Human Influence

Transported humans become ecological participants.

Examples include:

Cutting forests.

Building settlements.

Fishing.

Hunting.

Agriculture.

Domestication.

These activities permanently influence surrounding ecosystems.

Humanity becomes another ecological force.

---

# Ecological Recovery

Nature attempts to recover from disturbance.

Examples include:

Abandoned farms returning to grassland.

Ruined settlements becoming forests.

Animal populations recovering after overhunting.

Vegetation reclaiming roads.

Recovery occurs naturally over time.

---

# Extinction

Extinction is possible.

If a population reaches zero, that species may disappear from the region.

Whether complete global extinction is possible should remain a configurable simulation rule.

Extinction should never occur casually.

It should emerge only through prolonged ecological change.

---

# Carrying Capacity

Every ecosystem possesses finite resources.

Population growth eventually encounters natural limits.

Examples include:

Available food.

Water.

Habitat.

Competition.

Unlimited growth is impossible.

---

# Climate Interaction

Climate continuously influences ecology.

Examples include:

Rainfall encouraging vegetation.

Drought reducing plant growth.

Cold affecting migration.

Floods altering habitats.

Ecology responds naturally to environmental conditions.

---

# Seasons

Seasonal changes influence:

- breeding
- migration
- flowering
- fruit production
- hibernation
- food availability

No season should simply change visuals.

Every season changes ecological behavior.

---

# Simulation Rules

Every ecological system should satisfy the following principles.

## Independence

The ecosystem functions without players.

---

## Persistence

Life continues during player absence.

---

## Interconnection

Every species influences others.

---

## Adaptation

The ecosystem responds to changing conditions.

---

## Consequence

Player actions permanently influence ecological balance.

---

## Emergence

Interesting situations arise naturally from ecological interactions rather than scripted events.

---

# Final Principle

Project Draugr does not simulate a world filled with creatures.

It simulates a world filled with life.

Life survives.

Life competes.

Life adapts.

Whether humanity survives within that living world is another question entirely.
