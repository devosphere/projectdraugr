# 04 – Gameplay Systems

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *Living within the world.*

---

# Purpose

This document defines how players interact with the Project Draugr world.

Unlike traditional RPGs, gameplay systems are not isolated mechanics.

They are interfaces through which players participate in the living simulation.

The objective is not to create entertaining mechanics.

The objective is to create believable human existence within the world.

---

# Gameplay Philosophy

Players are not controlling heroes.

They are living ordinary human lives inside an extraordinary world.

Every gameplay system should reinforce:

- survival
- responsibility
- adaptation
- learning
- consequence

The player is never above the simulation.

They exist inside it.

---

# The Chronicle

Every player possesses one Chronicle.

A Chronicle represents an entire human life.

A Chronicle begins when the transported human awakens.

It ends permanently upon death.

The Chronicle records:

- discoveries
- settlements built
- structures created
- relationships
- journals
- achievements
- failures
- final cause of death

A Chronicle is not a save file.

It is history.

---

# Survival

Survival forms the foundation of gameplay.

Players must manage:

- hunger
- thirst
- fatigue
- body temperature
- injuries
- illness
- shelter
- nutrition

Failure to maintain these conditions eventually results in death.

There are no artificial game-over states.

Death occurs naturally through accumulated consequences.

---

# Knowledge

Knowledge is the primary progression system.

Players improve through experience rather than experience points.

Examples include:

Learning edible plants.

Learning dangerous animals.

Learning construction methods.

Learning farming techniques.

Learning metallurgy.

Learning medicine.

Learning navigation.

Knowledge becomes part of the player's Chronicle.

---

# Skills

Skills emerge naturally through repeated actions.

Examples:

Fishing improves by fishing.

Construction improves by building.

Cooking improves by cooking.

Tracking improves by hunting.

Medicine improves by treating injuries.

There are no skill trees.

No allocated points.

No arbitrary level requirements.

---

# Construction

Players may construct:

- camps
- shelters
- cabins
- farms
- storage
- workshops
- defensive structures
- roads
- bridges
- settlements

Construction permanently changes the world.

Structures become part of future Chronicles.

---

# Crafting

Crafting converts available resources into useful tools and equipment.

Examples include:

Weapons

Armor

Tools

Containers

Furniture

Cooking equipment

Storage systems

Construction materials

Crafting depends upon:

Knowledge.

Resources.

Tools.

Time.

---

# Hunting

Players may hunt wildlife.

Successful hunting requires:

Preparation.

Tracking.

Equipment.

Environmental understanding.

Poor decisions may result in injury or death.

---

# Gathering

Players may gather:

Plants.

Fruit.

Herbs.

Fiber.

Stone.

Clay.

Wood.

Water.

Gathering depends upon regional ecosystems and seasonal availability.

---

# Farming

Players may establish agriculture.

Examples include:

Food crops.

Medicinal herbs.

Tree cultivation.

Animal domestication.

Agriculture creates long-term sustainability rather than immediate survival.

---

# Animal Husbandry

Players may domesticate certain species.

Animals require:

Food.

Water.

Shelter.

Protection.

Neglect produces consequences.

Domestication becomes another part of civilization building.

---

# Combat

Combat exists as one survival tool among many.

It is never the primary progression system.

Players may encounter:

Wildlife.

Monsters.

Environmental hazards.

Other transported humans.

Preparation often determines victory before combat begins.

Avoiding combat is frequently the optimal decision.

---

# Health

The human body behaves realistically.

Players experience:

Fatigue.

Starvation.

Dehydration.

Disease.

Infection.

Blood loss.

Fractures.

Exposure.

Recovery requires time rather than instant healing.

---

# Inventory

Players possess limited carrying capacity.

Every item has:

Weight.

Volume.

Durability.

Purpose.

Players must make meaningful decisions regarding equipment and supplies.

---

# Equipment

Equipment supports survival.

Examples include:

Clothing.

Weapons.

Tools.

Backpacks.

Containers.

Fishing gear.

Construction equipment.

Medical supplies.

Every item possesses durability.

Maintenance extends useful life.

---

# Shelter

Shelter protects players from environmental hazards.

Examples include:

Weather.

Temperature.

Wildlife.

Monsters.

Shelters require maintenance.

Neglect eventually leads to deterioration.

---

# Fire

Fire is essential.

It provides:

Heat.

Cooking.

Light.

Protection.

Tool production.

Fire also introduces risk.

Uncontrolled fire may destroy player creations or surrounding ecosystems.

---

# Sleep

Sleep restores physical and mental condition.

Ignoring fatigue gradually reduces performance.

Extended sleep deprivation eventually becomes fatal.

The world continues during sleep.

---

# Responsibility

Players remain responsible for their lives while offline.

Examples include:

Food storage.

Fire safety.

Animal care.

Crop management.

Shelter security.

Poor preparation creates consequences.

---

# Death

Death permanently concludes a Chronicle.

No resurrection.

No loading previous saves.

No undo.

Everything created during life remains within the world whenever technically possible.

Death is not failure.

It is the natural conclusion of one existence.

---

# Legacy

The player's greatest achievement is not personal progression.

It is the permanent mark left upon the world.

Examples include:

A bridge.

A farm.

A settlement.

A journal.

A road.

A civilization.

A forgotten grave.

Future players inherit these contributions.

---

# Design Principles

Every gameplay mechanic should satisfy the following principles.

## Meaningful

Actions produce believable consequences.

---

## Persistent

Player actions permanently influence the world whenever technically possible.

---

## Knowledge Driven

Learning replaces artificial progression.

---

## Simulation First

Mechanics should emerge naturally from the simulation rather than override it.

---

## Responsibility

Players remain accountable for their decisions.

---

## Consequence

Success and failure both naturally emerge from player choices.

---

# Final Principle

Project Draugr is not about winning.

It is not about reaching maximum level.

It is not about completing quests.

It is about living one human life within a world that continues long after that life ends.

The Chronicle eventually concludes.

The world continues.
