# 03 – World Simulation

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *A world that continues without you.*

---

# Purpose

This document defines how the Project Draugr world behaves over time.

Unlike traditional games where the world waits for the player, Project Draugr continuously evolves regardless of player presence.

Every system within the world participates in an interconnected simulation.

Nothing exists in complete isolation.

---

# Simulation Philosophy

Project Draugr is not event-driven.

It is state-driven.

The world is never asking:

> "What should happen because the player arrived?"

Instead it is constantly asking:

> "Given the current state of the world, what naturally happens next?"

The player is simply another participant within that simulation.

---

# Living World

Every region continues functioning continuously.

Examples include:

- weather changes
- vegetation grows
- vegetation dies
- rivers continue flowing
- lakes change water level
- wildlife migrates
- monsters relocate
- predators hunt prey
- prey reproduce
- resources regenerate
- abandoned structures decay

The simulation never pauses.

---

# Ecology

Every ecosystem attempts to maintain its own equilibrium.

Population naturally fluctuates.

Examples:

Large predator populations reduce herbivores.

Fewer herbivores increase vegetation.

Dense vegetation supports more herbivores.

More herbivores support more predators.

No species exists independently.

Everything participates within the ecological cycle.

---

# Wildlife Simulation

Every wildlife species follows programmed behavior.

Examples include:

- feeding
- drinking
- sleeping
- reproduction
- migration
- avoiding predators
- defending territory
- seasonal adaptation

Wildlife does not require player interaction to survive.

Players merely encounter ongoing lives.

---

# Monster Simulation

Monsters follow ecological behaviors appropriate to their species.

Examples include:

- territorial expansion
- hunting
- scavenging
- migration
- nesting
- reproduction (where applicable)
- conflict with rival monsters

Monsters are not scripted encounters.

They naturally occupy the world.

---

# Resource Simulation

Natural resources regenerate according to ecological conditions.

Examples include:

Trees mature over time.

Berry bushes produce seasonal fruit.

Medicinal herbs spread.

Animal populations recover.

Fish reproduce.

Ore does not regenerate.

Some resources renew.

Some are finite.

Players must learn the difference.

---

# Weather Simulation

Weather continuously changes according to regional climate.

Weather influences:

- visibility
- temperature
- wildlife activity
- vegetation
- water availability
- fire spread
- travel
- survival

Weather is never generated specifically for a player.

---

# Seasonal Simulation

The world progresses through real-world seasons.

Seasonal changes influence:

- plant growth
- harvest periods
- migration
- breeding seasons
- temperature
- daylight duration
- food availability

Players must adapt.

The seasons will not adapt to players.

---

# Construction Simulation

Player-built structures become permanent world objects.

Structures possess condition.

Examples:

- excellent
- maintained
- aging
- deteriorating
- ruined

Structures naturally deteriorate over time.

Maintenance slows deterioration.

Neglect accelerates it.

---

# Civilization Simulation

Human civilization evolves entirely through player actions.

Civilizations naturally experience:

- expansion
- resource consumption
- infrastructure growth
- abandonment
- decline
- collapse

The simulation never guarantees permanence.

Even great civilizations eventually become history.

---

# Historical Persistence

Every meaningful event contributes to world history.

Examples include:

Construction.

Destruction.

Settlement founding.

Settlement abandonment.

Major discoveries.

Deaths.

Natural disasters.

History accumulates.

It is never erased.

---

# Offline Continuity

Players remain responsible for their lives while offline.

Examples:

Food may spoil.

Fires extinguish.

Animals escape.

Structures deteriorate.

Crops mature.

Crops die.

Predators enter abandoned settlements.

Preparation determines survival.

---

# Cause And Effect

Every meaningful action has consequences.

Examples:

Heavy logging reduces forest density.

Overhunting reduces animal populations.

Large fires alter ecosystems.

Extensive farming changes local vegetation.

Building roads influences future travel.

The world always remembers accumulated change.

---

# World Equilibrium

The simulation naturally attempts to preserve long-term stability.

No system seeks perfect balance.

Instead, systems continuously respond to changing conditions.

Examples include:

Wildlife populations adapting.

Vegetation reclaiming abandoned settlements.

Predators moving toward abundant prey.

Natural succession restoring damaged environments.

Equilibrium is never static.

It is continuous adaptation.

---

# Creation And Destruction

The world's evolution reflects two primordial principles.

Creation allows new life, growth, construction, and renewal.

Destruction allows decay, death, collapse, and transformation.

Neither principle directly manipulates the world.

Instead, the simulation itself naturally expresses their eternal balance.

Players experience only observable consequences.

The principles themselves remain unseen.

---

# The Overseer

The Overseer never participates within the simulation.

It performs no balancing.

No intervention.

No correction.

Its purpose is preservation.

Every Chronicle.

Every civilization.

Every discovery.

Every death.

Becomes part of the world's permanent memory.

---

# Simulation Before Gameplay

Gameplay mechanics should emerge naturally from simulation whenever possible.

Examples:

Players do not receive a quest because wolves are nearby.

Players discover wolves because wolves naturally established territory there.

Players do not receive missions to build roads.

Roads emerge because civilization eventually requires them.

Simulation creates opportunity.

Players create stories.

---

# Design Principles

Every new simulation system should satisfy the following principles.

## Independence

The system functions without player interaction.

---

## Persistence

Its state continues evolving through time.

---

## Interconnection

Its behavior influences other systems.

---

## Predictability

Players may learn its rules through observation.

---

## Emergence

Interesting situations arise naturally rather than through scripted events.

---

## Continuity

The world never pauses.

The simulation always continues.

---

# Final Principle

The simulation is the heart of Project Draugr.

Players are not playing inside a scripted game.

They are living within a world that continues to exist whether they are present or not.

Everything else is built upon this principle.
