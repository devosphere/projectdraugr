# 18.7 – Knowledge UI

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *Knowledge is never given. It is earned.*

---

# Purpose

The Knowledge UI allows the player to review what the Chronicle has genuinely learned throughout its lifetime.

Knowledge in Project Draugr is not a skill tree, technology tree, or progression menu.

It is a living record of the Chronicle's accumulated understanding of the world.

The Knowledge UI reflects experience—not levels.

---

# Design Philosophy

Project Draugr follows one immutable principle:

> **The Chronicle only knows what it has personally learned.**

Knowledge is never unlocked because of:

- player level
- experience points
- achievements
- arbitrary progression

Knowledge is acquired through:

- observation
- experimentation
- repetition
- study
- instruction
- discovery

---

# Opening the Knowledge UI

The Knowledge UI appears only when intentionally requested.

Examples:

```text id="gtv17j"
Open knowledge.

Show knowledge.

Review knowledge.
```

The UI never opens automatically.

# Categories

Knowledge is organized by subject rather than progression.

Core categories include:

- Survival
- Craftsmanship
- Construction
- Flora
- Fauna
- Monsters
- Medicine
- Geography

Additional categories may naturally emerge as civilizations evolve.

---

# Knowledge Entries

Each entry represents genuine understanding.

Example:

```text id="r6dfc6"
Red Cap Mushroom

Status

Known

Description

Edible after cooking.

Often found beneath oak trees.
```

Without prior discovery, the entry simply does not exist.

---

# Progressive Understanding

Knowledge deepens over time.

Example:

First encounter:

```text id="4m7fqx"
Unknown Plant
```

After observation:

```text id="9j3fc3"
Red Plant

Produces edible berries.
```

After repeated study:

```text id="y5zykp"
Red Cap Berry Bush

Fruit ripens during late summer.

Leaves may be brewed into tea.

Seeds germinate best in moist soil.
```

The object itself never changes.

Only the Chronicle's understanding evolves.

---

# Knowledge Sources

Knowledge may originate from:

Observation

Example:

Watching an animal build a nest.

---

Experimentation

Example:

Testing whether an unknown fruit is edible.

---

Repetition

Example:

Repeatedly weaving rope until proficiency improves.

---

Teaching

Example:

Another Chronicle demonstrates pottery.

---

Written Records

Example:

Reading an old manuscript.

---

Discovery

Example:

Finding ancient ruins containing forgotten techniques.

---

# Hidden Knowledge

Unknown information remains completely absent.

The UI never hints at undiscovered entries.

Example:

If the Chronicle has never encountered wolves:

There is no empty entry saying:

```text id="ugjlwm"
???

Unknown Creature
```

Nothing appears.

The player should never know something exists merely because the interface suggests it.

---

Flora

Fauna

---

Monsters

Knowledge changes perception—not reality.

---

# Chronicle Specific

Knowledge belongs to the Chronicle.

When a Chronicle dies:

Its knowledge dies with it.

Future Chronicles begin with only the knowledge appropriate to their own background and experiences.

The world may preserve books, journals, and notes, but understanding them still requires learning.

---

# Legacy

Knowledge may be preserved physically.

Examples:

- journals
- books
- diagrams
- maps
- engineering notes
- laboratory records

Future Chronicles may recover these artifacts and learn from them.

Knowledge itself is not inherited.

Only its records survive.

---

# Future Expansion

The Knowledge UI may later support:

- handwritten notes
- bookmarking
- illustrations
- personal observations
- research journals
- cross-references
- collaborative discoveries
- libraries

These additions must preserve the same philosophy.

---

# Final Principle

The Knowledge UI is not an encyclopedia.

It is the Chronicle's memory.

The interface never teaches.

It only records what has already been earned through experience.

The world remains full of mysteries.

Only exploration turns them into knowledge.
