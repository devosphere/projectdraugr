# 15 – Infrastructure

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *Civilizations are not built by tools alone, but by the places where tools are used.*

---

# Purpose

Infrastructure represents physical improvements to the world that increase efficiency, organization, productivity, and quality of life.

Unlike crafted items, infrastructure is not simply something a Chronicle owns.

It is something that **changes how work is performed**.

Infrastructure transforms survival into civilization.

---

# Design Philosophy

Project Draugr follows one immutable principle:

> **Infrastructure improves the environment—not the character.**

There are no magical crafting stations.

There are no arbitrary technology unlocks.

Infrastructure exists because humans naturally build better environments to perform work.

A flat stone becomes a work surface.

A work surface becomes a woodworking bench.

A bench becomes a workshop.

A workshop becomes an industry.

---

# Definition

Infrastructure is any constructed object or structure whose primary purpose is to improve one or more of the following:

- efficiency
- precision
- safety
- organization
- productivity
- comfort
- scalability

Infrastructure does not exist merely to satisfy game mechanics.

It exists because it would naturally improve human work.

# Infrastructure Effects

Infrastructure modifies reality.

It never grants artificial bonuses.

Instead, it changes the working conditions.

Example:

Woodworking Table

Effects

- Stable working surface
- Better posture
- Improved cutting precision
- Reduced fatigue
- Reduced crafting time
- Lower material waste
- Higher consistency

---

Stone Working Table

Effects

- Stable striking surface
- Better leverage
- More accurate shaping
- Lower accidental fractures

---

Weaving Frame

Effects

- Maintains thread tension
- Frees both hands
- Faster weaving
- More consistent fabric

---

Sewing Table

Effects

- Better posture
- Stable workspace
- Easier organization of tools
- Improved stitching accuracy

---

Drying Rack

Effects

- Improves airflow
- Reduces spoilage
- Better preservation
- Prevents ground contamination

# Workspaces

Multiple infrastructure objects naturally combine into workspaces.

Example

Carpenter's Workshop

Contains

- Woodworking Table
- Work Stool
- Tool Rack
- Timber Storage
- Drying Rack

The workshop itself is not a magical entity.

Its effectiveness emerges from the combination of its infrastructure.

---

# Accessibility

Infrastructure must physically exist.

A Chronicle may only benefit from infrastructure that is:

- nearby
- accessible
- functional

A woodworking table left behind at another settlement provides no benefit until the Chronicle returns.

---

# Persistence

Infrastructure permanently exists within the world.

Unless destroyed, it remains after:

- player logout
- Chronicle death
- abandonment
- ownership changes

Future Chronicles may discover abandoned workshops and continue using them.

---

# Knowledge

Building infrastructure requires knowledge.

Knowing how to build a woodworking table is separate from knowing how to craft a wooden spear.

Knowledge determines what infrastructure can be designed.

Resources determine whether it can be built.

---

Infrastructure may later support:

- collaborative construction
- maintenance
- degradation
- upgrades
- modular expansion
- automation
- energy systems
- public infrastructure

---

# Final Principle

Infrastructure does not make people better.

It allows people to work better.

Project Draugr measures civilization not by levels or technology trees, but by the environments players create.

A civilization is remembered not by the tools it held, but by the places it built.
