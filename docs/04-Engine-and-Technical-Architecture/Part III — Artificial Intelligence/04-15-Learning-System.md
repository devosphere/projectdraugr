# Purpose

The Learning System defines how autonomous entities improve over time through experience, observation, experimentation, instruction, and cultural exchange.

Learning transforms experience into capability.

Without learning, entities remain static.

With learning, individuals, societies, and civilizations evolve continuously throughout the world's history.

---

# Design Philosophy

Learning is not gaining experience points.

Learning is changing behavior because of experience.

Every success, failure, observation, conversation, and discovery has the potential to reshape future decisions.

Knowledge accumulates.

Skills develop.

Habits form.

Civilizations advance.

---

# Core Principles

## Experience Creates Learning

Only lived or observed experiences can produce meaningful learning.

---

## Learning is Gradual

Improvement occurs through repeated reinforcement rather than instantaneous mastery.

---

## Failure is Valuable

Mistakes are an essential source of learning.

---

## Learning Never Ends

Entities continue learning throughout their lives.

---

## Knowledge Spreads

Learning may transfer between individuals, communities, and generations.

---

# Responsibilities

The Learning System is responsible for:

- acquiring knowledge;
- improving skills;
- adapting behavior;
- forming habits;
- observational learning;
- teaching and education;
- cultural transmission;
- behavioral evolution.

It is not responsible for:

- storing memories;
- executing actions;
- selecting goals.

---

# Learning Lifecycle

Every learning event follows a common progression.

```text
Experience

↓

Observation

↓

Reflection

↓

Knowledge Integration

↓

Behavior Adaptation

↓

Repetition

↓

Mastery

↓

Teaching
```

Learning is continuous rather than event-based.

---

# Sources of Learning

Entities learn through multiple channels.

---

## Direct Experience

Personal success and failure.

Examples:

- surviving a hunt;
- forging a weapon;
- becoming injured.

Direct experience produces the strongest learning.

---

## Observation

Watching others.

Examples:

- observing a blacksmith;
- watching predators hunt;
- witnessing battles.

Observation accelerates learning.

---

## Instruction

Learning from teachers.

Examples:

- apprenticeships;
- schools;
- military drills;
- magical academies.

Instruction transfers structured knowledge.

---

## Experimentation

Learning through trial and error.

Examples:

- mixing herbs;
- testing alloys;
- exploring unknown ruins;
- magical research.

Experimentation creates entirely new knowledge.

---

## Social Exchange

Conversation spreads information.

Examples:

- travelers;
- merchants;
- scholars;
- storytellers.

Information may be accurate or incorrect.

---

# Skill Improvement

Repeated practice gradually improves proficiency.

General progression:

```text
Novice

↓

Beginner

↓

Competent

↓

Skilled

↓

Expert

↓

Master
```

Advancement depends upon meaningful practice rather than repetition alone.

---

# Knowledge Growth

Knowledge expands through connected understanding.

Example:

```text
Fire

↓

Cooking

↓

Food Preservation

↓

Trade

↓

Population Growth
```

Knowledge networks become increasingly sophisticated.

---

# Habit Formation

Frequently repeated behaviors become habits.

Examples:

- checking weapons before travel;
- greeting neighbors;
- washing wounds;
- conserving food.

Habits reduce cognitive effort for routine tasks.

---

# Behavioral Adaptation

Entities modify future behavior according to previous outcomes.

Example:

```text
Cross River During Storm

↓

Nearly Drown

↓

Avoid River During Storms
```

Adaptation improves long-term survival.

---

# Learning Rate

Learning speed depends upon factors such as:

- intelligence;
- age;
- repetition;
- teaching quality;
- motivation;
- physical condition;
- available resources.

Not all entities learn equally.

---

# Knowledge Decay

Unused knowledge may gradually weaken.

Examples:

- forgotten languages;
- neglected crafts;
- outdated military tactics.

Continued practice preserves competence.

---

# Innovation

Learning may produce entirely new ideas.

Examples:

- improved farming methods;
- stronger alloys;
- advanced architecture;
- medical discoveries.

Innovation emerges from combining existing knowledge in novel ways.

---

# Teaching

Experienced entities may teach others.

Teaching quality depends upon:

- expertise;
- communication ability;
- patience;
- available time.

Teaching accelerates societal development.

---

# Institutional Learning

Organizations preserve accumulated knowledge.

Examples:

- libraries;
- guilds;
- universities;
- monasteries.

Institutions enable knowledge to outlive individuals.

---

# Generational Learning

Knowledge transfers across generations.

Examples:

- family traditions;
- apprenticeships;
- written records;
- oral histories.

Civilizations advance because knowledge survives individual lifetimes.

---

# Collective Learning

Groups may improve together.

Examples:

- military units refining tactics;
- villages improving agriculture;
- merchants developing trade routes.

Collective learning creates emergent societal progress.

---

# Failure Recovery

Repeated failure encourages adaptation.

Examples:

- redesigning tools;
- abandoning ineffective strategies;
- seeking expert guidance.

Persistent failure without adaptation is considered poor learning.

---

# Integration with Memory

Memory stores experiences.

Learning extracts patterns from those experiences.

Memory answers:

> "What happened?"

Learning answers:

> "What does it mean?"

---

# Integration with Decision-Making

Learning continuously modifies future decision weights.

Experienced entities evaluate situations differently from inexperienced ones.

---

# Integration with Goal Planning

Learning expands available planning options.

As knowledge grows, entities become capable of pursuing increasingly complex goals.

---

# Debug Support

Development tools should visualize:

- learning progress;
- skill development;
- knowledge graph expansion;
- habit formation;
- teaching relationships;
- innovation history;
- forgotten competencies.

Developers should be able to trace why an entity became more capable over time.

---

# Performance Considerations

The Learning System should optimize:

- incremental updates;
- event-driven learning;
- compressed knowledge representation;
- scalable institutional archives;
- adaptive simulation fidelity.

Inactive entities should continue slow long-term learning through reduced simulation models.

---

# Developer Notes

Learning is the engine of civilization.

Without learning, the world merely repeats itself.

With learning, every generation has the potential to surpass the previous one.

Project Draugr should allow players to witness societies genuinely evolving—not because scripted eras unlock, but because countless individuals gradually become more capable and pass their understanding to others.

The objective is not simply smarter AI.

The objective is a world that becomes wiser.
