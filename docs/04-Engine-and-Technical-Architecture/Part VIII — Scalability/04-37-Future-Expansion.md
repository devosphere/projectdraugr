# Purpose

The Future Expansion architecture defines how the Project Draugr engine is expected to evolve over the coming years without compromising existing Chronicles, simulation integrity, or architectural philosophy.

Unlike most engines, Project Draugr is not designed for a single game.

It is designed to become the permanent technological foundation upon which an ever-evolving living universe is built.

Future technologies should integrate into the engine.

The engine should never require reinvention because technology has advanced.

---

# Design Philosophy

Technology changes.

Architecture endures.

Every decision made today should reduce the cost of tomorrow's innovation.

The engine should continuously evolve while preserving the continuity of every Chronicle ever created.

---

# Core Principles

## Evolution Without Replacement

Subsystems should be replaceable without redesigning the entire engine.

---

## Stable Foundations

Core architectural contracts should remain stable across engine generations.

---

## Incremental Innovation

Large architectural changes should occur through gradual evolution rather than disruptive rewrites.

---

## Chronicle Preservation

Technological progress must never invalidate existing worlds.

---

## Platform Independence

The engine should adapt to future hardware instead of depending upon today's hardware assumptions.

---

# Architectural Layers

Future expansion follows layered evolution.

```text
Vision

↓

Simulation Architecture

↓

Engine Architecture

↓

Subsystems

↓

Implementations

↓

Hardware
```

Higher layers change more slowly than lower layers.

---

# Stable Contracts

The following architectural contracts should remain stable whenever possible:

- entity identity;
- simulation tick model;
- Chronicle format;
- world rules;
- deterministic execution;
- serialization contracts;
- engine APIs.

Stable contracts preserve long-term compatibility.

---

# Replaceable Implementations

Subsystem implementations may evolve independently.

Examples include:

- rendering backends;
- physics engines;
- AI models;
- networking protocols;
- storage engines;
- asset pipelines.

Implementations are replaceable.

Architecture is not.

- biological evolution;
- economic forecasting;
- machine learning;
- new rendering technologies.

Modules minimize architectural disruption.
