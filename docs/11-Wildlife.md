# 11 – Wildlife

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *Animals are not resources. They are lives sharing the same world.*

---

# Purpose

This document defines the wildlife system of Project Draugr.

Wildlife represents naturally occurring animal life inhabiting the world.

Animals exist independently of players and fulfill ecological roles within their respective environments.

Their purpose is not to provide food or gameplay.

They simply live.

---

# Design Philosophy

Wildlife are living organisms.

They possess needs.

They make decisions.

They react to the environment.

They reproduce.

They compete.

They migrate.

They die.

Players are simply another species entering their world.

---

# Wildlife Classification

Wildlife are classified according to ecological behavior rather than gameplay difficulty.

---

## Herbivores

Animals primarily consuming vegetation.

Examples:

- Deer
- Elk
- Rabbits
- Wild Boar
- Mountain Goats

Primary ecological role:

Convert plant matter into animal biomass.

---

## Omnivores

Animals consuming both plants and animals.

Examples:

- Wild Boars
- Bears
- Certain Birds
- Small Mammals

Primary ecological role:

Ecological generalists.

---

## Carnivores

Predatory animals primarily consuming other animals.

Examples:

- Wolves
- Wildcats
- Large Raptors

Primary ecological role:

Population regulation.

---

## Scavengers

Animals feeding upon dead organisms.

Examples:

- Vultures
- Crows
- Hyenas (region dependent)

Primary ecological role:

Nutrient recycling.

---

## Aquatic Wildlife

Freshwater and marine organisms.

Examples:

- Fish
- Turtles
- Frogs
- Crustaceans

Primary ecological role:

Aquatic ecosystem stability.

---

## Small Creatures

Small organisms supporting ecosystem diversity.

Examples:

- Insects
- Rodents
- Reptiles
- Amphibians

Though individually insignificant, they collectively sustain larger ecosystems.

---

# Behavior

Wildlife behavior is determined through programmed survival systems.

Animals naturally:

- search for food
- drink
- rest
- avoid danger
- defend themselves
- establish territory
- reproduce
- raise offspring
- migrate when necessary

Behavior is driven by survival rather than scripted events.

---

# Daily Activity

Most species follow daily activity cycles.

Examples include:

- diurnal
- nocturnal
- crepuscular

Activity patterns influence encounters naturally.

---

# Hunger

Animals consume food appropriate to their species.

Food availability directly influences:

- health
- reproduction
- migration
- population growth

---

# Thirst

Animals require water.

Water sources influence:

- migration routes
- territories
- seasonal behavior

---

# Sleep

Animals require rest.

Sleeping locations vary naturally according to species.

Examples:

- nests
- burrows
- dens
- trees
- caves

---

# Territory

Many species establish territories.

Territories provide:

- feeding areas
- breeding grounds
- shelter

Territorial conflicts occur naturally.

---

# Reproduction

Species reproduce according to biological characteristics.

Reproduction depends upon:

- maturity
- food availability
- environmental conditions
- breeding season

Population growth should emerge naturally.

---

# Offspring

Young animals remain vulnerable.

Parents may:

- defend offspring
- teach movement
- remain nearby

Offspring eventually mature into independent adults.

---

# Mortality

Wildlife die naturally.

Causes include:

- predation
- starvation
- disease
- old age
- accidents
- environmental hazards

Death forms part of normal ecological balance.

---

# Fear

Animals evaluate threats.

Possible responses include:

- fleeing
- hiding
- warning
- defending territory
- protecting offspring
- attacking when cornered

Not every species reacts identically.

---

# Curiosity

Some wildlife investigate unfamiliar objects.

Others avoid them entirely.

Behavior varies naturally by species.

---

# Human Interaction

Animals gradually learn from repeated encounters.

Examples:

Avoiding dangerous locations.

Changing migration routes.

Becoming accustomed to settlements.

Avoiding traps.

The world remembers human behavior through ecological adaptation rather than artificial scripting.

---

# Domestication

Certain wildlife may eventually become domesticated.

Domestication is never immediate.

It requires:

- trust
- repeated interaction
- care
- successful breeding

Domestication should emerge gradually over multiple generations whenever appropriate.

---

# Population

Every wildlife species maintains regional populations.

Population changes through:

- births
- deaths
- migration
- food availability
- disease

No population remains permanently fixed.

---

# Extinction

Regional extinction is possible.

Complete extinction should remain rare.

Recovery depends upon:

- remaining populations
- migration
- environmental suitability

Nature continually attempts to restore equilibrium.

---

# Human Impact

Transported humans become another ecological influence.

Examples include:

- hunting
- trapping
- habitat destruction
- domestication
- agriculture

Wildlife gradually adapts to these changes.

---

# Observation

Watching wildlife is valuable.

Players may discover:

- migration routes
- watering locations
- breeding grounds
- feeding behavior
- predator territories

Observation often proves more valuable than immediate hunting.

---

# Species Diversity

Different geographical regions support different wildlife.

No single region contains every species.

Exploration reveals new ecosystems.

---

# Wildlife Records

Every discovered species should become permanently recorded within the player's Chronicle.

Records may include:

- appearance
- habitat
- behavior
- diet
- danger level
- useful resources
- personal observations

Knowledge accumulates through experience.

---

# Design Principles

Wildlife systems should satisfy the following principles.

## Alive

Animals behave like living organisms.

---

## Independent

Wildlife exists without player participation.

---

## Adaptive

Behavior changes according to experience and environmental conditions.

---

## Ecological

Every species fulfills a role.

---

## Persistent

Populations continue evolving while players are offline.

---

## Believable

Animals prioritize survival over combat.

---

# Final Principle

Wildlife are not enemies.

They are not allies.

They are not collectibles.

They are fellow inhabitants of an ancient world.

Long before humanity arrived, they lived.

Long after a Chronicle ends, they will continue living.

Players are merely temporary visitors within their home.
