# 01 – Vision Bible

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *Every life leaves a mark.*

---

# Purpose

This document defines the immutable creative vision of Project Draugr.

Every future gameplay system, technical implementation, content expansion, and design decision must remain faithful to the principles documented here.

Technology may evolve.

The implementation may evolve.

The vision must not.

---

# Vision Statement

Project Draugr is a persistent world simulation where human civilization begins with the players.

The world existed long before humanity arrived.

It will continue existing long after every Chronicle has ended.

Players are not heroes.

They are not chosen.

They are ordinary humans suddenly transported into an unknown world where survival, adaptation, and consequence define their existence.

The purpose of Project Draugr is not to tell stories.

It is to create a world where stories naturally emerge.

---

# The World Is The Protagonist

Unlike traditional RPGs, Project Draugr is not centered around the player.

The player is only one existence within a much larger world.

The mountains do not exist because players explore them.

The forests do not exist because players gather wood.

The rivers do not flow because players need water.

The wildlife does not exist to become loot.

The monsters do not exist to become bosses.

The world exists independently.

Players simply become part of it.

Every design decision should reinforce this philosophy.

---

# Humanity Begins With The Player

Project Draugr contains no living human civilization.

There are:

- no kingdoms
- no villages
- no towns
- no cities
- no merchants
- no blacksmiths
- no rulers
- no political factions
- no quest givers

Human civilization begins only when transported humans decide to build it.

Every camp.

Every bridge.

Every farm.

Every road.

Every village.

Every city.

Every kingdom.

Every empire.

Exists because one or more Chronicles created it.

Civilization itself is emergent gameplay.

---

# The Ancient World

Although humanity is absent, the world is not empty.

It contains:

- forests
- rivers
- lakes
- mountains
- caves
- plains
- swamps
- oceans
- wildlife
- monsters
- forgotten ruins
- abandoned structures
- relics
- mysterious artifacts

Evidence suggests that intelligent civilizations once existed.

The world remembers them only through ruins, architecture, relics, and forgotten history.

Whether those civilizations destroyed themselves, transcended existence, or simply vanished remains intentionally unknown.

The player is not arriving at the beginning of history.

They are arriving after countless forgotten ages.

---

# One Persistent World

There is only one canonical world.

Every Chronicle shares this world.

Its geography never resets.

Its history is cumulative.

Its ecosystems continue evolving.

Buildings remain.

Roads remain.

Settlements remain.

Graves remain.

Ruins remain.

Whenever technically possible, the world remembers everything players leave behind.

Players inherit the consequences of those who came before them.

---

# One Chronicle

Each player possesses only one active Chronicle.

Death permanently concludes that Chronicle.

There are no save reloads.

No resurrection.

No alternate timelines.

When a player dies, another ordinary human may someday awaken within the same world.

History continues.

The world remembers.

---

# Identity Through Action

Project Draugr rejects predefined identities.

Players never choose:

- classes
- professions
- races
- backgrounds
- skill trees

Identity emerges naturally through repetition.

Those who hunt become hunters.

Those who farm become farmers.

Those who build become builders.

Those who explore become explorers.

Knowledge replaces experience points.

Practice replaces levels.

The world recognizes capability—not labels.

---

# Persistence Over Progression

Traditional RPGs focus on character progression.

Project Draugr focuses on world progression.

The most important progression belongs to the world itself.

Forests mature.

Settlements expand.

Roads connect distant regions.

Civilizations rise.

Civilizations collapse.

Ruins accumulate.

History grows.

Players contribute to this evolution.

They never control it.

---

# The Living Simulation

Project Draugr is fundamentally a simulation.

The world continues evolving regardless of player presence.

Wildlife migrates.

Monster territories shift.

Vegetation grows.

Vegetation dies.

Resources regenerate.

Weather changes.

Seasons pass.

Civilizations created by players evolve through time.

The player experiences a world already in motion.

---

# Real-Time Continuity

Time within Project Draugr progresses alongside reality.

The in-game calendar mirrors the real-world calendar.

The in-game clock mirrors the real-world clock.

The world never pauses.

Before leaving, players are responsible for preparing their lives for their absence.

Food spoils.

Fires extinguish.

Animals wander.

Structures deteriorate.

A well-managed settlement may survive weeks.

A poorly prepared camp may disappear overnight.

Responsibility continues even while the player is away.

---

# Invisible Existence

Not every existence belongs to the physical world.

Beyond ordinary perception exist two primordial principles.

## Creation

Creation expands existence.

It gives rise to life.

Growth.

Formation.

Discovery.

Construction.

Evolution.

Creation is not kindness.

Unchecked Creation would eventually destroy existence.

---

## Destruction

Destruction renews existence.

It brings decay.

Death.

Collapse.

Fire.

Erosion.

Extinction.

Destruction is not cruelty.

Unchecked Destruction would also destroy existence.

---

## Existence

Existence is born from the eternal balance between Creation and Destruction.

Neither principle seeks victory.

Neither seeks dominance.

If either were ever to completely overwhelm the other, existence itself would cease.

Whether these primordial principles are beings, intelligences, or something beyond mortal understanding has never been confirmed.

Players never witness them directly.

Most players will never know they exist.

The world simply responds to their eternal equilibrium.

---

# The Overseer

Beyond the world exists only one observer.

The Overseer.

The Overseer never intervenes.

It never commands.

It never rescues.

It never rewards.

It never punishes.

It remembers.

Every Chronicle.

Every discovery.

Every settlement.

Every failure.

Every death.

The true purpose of the Overseer remains unknown.

---

# History Is The Greatest Treasure

The greatest reward in Project Draugr is not equipment.

It is legacy.

A forgotten cabin.

An abandoned journal.

A ruined fortress.

An ancient bridge.

A weathered grave.

A once-great city reclaimed by nature.

These become historical artifacts for future Chronicles.

Every player inherits the world left behind by previous lives.

Every player leaves the world changed for those who come after.

---

# Design Philosophy

Every new feature should answer one question:

> **Does this make the world feel more alive without depending on the player?**

If the answer is yes, it belongs in Project Draugr.

If it merely makes the player stronger, richer, or more convenient while weakening the world's independence, it should be reconsidered.

The player should always feel like a participant within the world—not the reason it exists.

The world always comes first.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Project Draugr is designed to evolve over many years.

The first generations of the project focus on building a believable persistent world simulation.

Future technological advancements may introduce increasingly intelligent existences, richer ecological systems, deeper civilization simulation, and more sophisticated autonomous behaviors.

Artificial intelligence is an evolutionary enhancement.

It is not the foundation of the project.

The world must first feel alive before it becomes intelligent.

Regardless of technological evolution, one principle must never change:

> **The world does not exist for the player.**

> **The player becomes part of the world.**

---

# Final Principle

Project Draugr is not about saving the world.

It is not about becoming the strongest.

It is not about defeating an ultimate evil.

It is about existing within a world that neither knows nor needs you.

Every Chronicle answers only one question:

> **Who do you become when everything familiar is taken away?**

The answer is never written by the developers.

It is written by the player's life.

Every life leaves a mark.

The world remembers.

The Overseer never forgets.
