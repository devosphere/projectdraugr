# 18.2 – Body HUD

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *The body knows before the mind understands.*

---

# Purpose

The Body HUD represents the internal physical awareness of the player's Chronicle.

In reality, humans constantly receive biological feedback without consciously thinking about it.

Examples include:

- hunger
- thirst
- fatigue
- body temperature
- pain
- bladder pressure
- cleanliness
- wet clothing
- illness
- injury

Because Project Draugr is a text-based simulation, the player cannot physically experience these sensations.

The Body HUD exists to replace those missing biological senses.

It provides truthful awareness of the Chronicle's current physical condition.

---

# Design Philosophy

The Body HUD follows one immutable principle:

> **The HUD informs. The player decides.**

The Body HUD never:

- recommends actions
- warns the player repeatedly
- suggests solutions
- protects the player from poor decisions

It only reports the body's current condition.

Survival depends on how the player interprets that information.

# Body Information Categories

## Health

Represents the overall survivability of the Chronicle.

Examples:

```
Healthy

Injured

Wounded

Critical

Dying
```

Health is affected by:

- injuries
- blood loss
- illness
- poisoning
- environmental exposure
- untreated conditions

---

## Condition

Represents specific abnormalities affecting the body.

Examples:

```
Minor Cut

Bruised Rib

Broken Arm

Food Poisoning

Fever

Infection

Hypothermia

Venomous Bite
```

Multiple conditions may exist simultaneously.

Example:

```
Condition

Broken Arm
Infected Wound
High Fever
```

---

## Hunger

Represents nutritional state.

Examples:

```
Full

Satisfied

Hungry

Very Hungry

Starving
```

For the MVP, hunger progresses using simplified constant thresholds documented separately.

---

## Thirst

Represents hydration.

Examples:

```
Hydrated

Thirsty

Very Thirsty

Dehydrated

Severely Dehydrated
```

For the MVP, thirst progresses using simplified constant thresholds documented separately.

---

## Energy

Represents physical exhaustion.

Examples:

```
Energetic

Rested

Tired

Fatigued

Exhausted

Collapsing
```

Energy affects:

- movement
- crafting
- combat
- recovery
- physical performance

---

## Temperature

Represents the body's thermal comfort.

Examples:

```
Cold

Comfortable

Warm

Hot

Hypothermic

Hyperthermic
```

Temperature depends on:

- weather
- clothing
- shelter
- fire
- wetness
- environmental exposure

---

## Wetness

Represents how wet the Chronicle's body and clothing are.

Examples:

```
Dry

Damp

Wet

Soaked
```

Wetness influences:

- body temperature
- comfort
- drying time
- disease risk (future)

Sources include:

- rain
- rivers
- lakes
- swimming
- storms

---

## Bladder

Represents urinary urgency.

Examples:

```
Empty

Comfortable

Need to Urinate

Urgent

Critical
```

Ignoring bladder needs may eventually lead to involuntary loss of control.

The HUD exists so the narrator never has to repeatedly remind the player.

---

## Hygiene

Represents bodily cleanliness.

Examples:

```
Clean

Normal

Dirty

Filthy

Hazardous
```

Hygiene gradually changes through:

- sweat
- mud
- blood
- environmental exposure
- lack of washing

Future systems may use hygiene for:

- disease risk
- odor
- infection probability
- social interactions

---

# Knowledge and Diagnosis

The Body HUD always reveals that something is wrong.

Understanding depends on the Chronicle's knowledge.

Example:

A survivor without medical knowledge may see:

```
Condition

Unknown Fever
```

A trained healer may see:

```
Condition

Respiratory Infection
```

A master physician may see:

```
Condition

Severe respiratory infection.
High fever.
Elevated complication risk.
```

The body does not change.

Only interpretation improves.

---

# No Numerical Values

The Body HUD intentionally avoids numerical optimization.

Incorrect:

```
Health: 72%
Thirst: 45%
Temperature: 36.9°C
```

Correct:

```
Health: Wounded
Thirst: Very Thirsty
Temperature: Cold
```

Players should think like humans—not spreadsheets.

---

# Relationship With Narration

The narrator does not proactively repeat Body HUD information.

Incorrect:

HUD

```
Thirst
Very Thirsty
```

Narrator

> "You are very thirsty."

Correct:

HUD

```
Thirst
Very Thirsty
```

Narrator

> "Your lips feel dry as you continue walking through the afternoon heat."

The narrator describes observable consequences.

The Body HUD communicates internal awareness.

---

# Player Responsibility

The simulation never assumes the player noticed the Body HUD.

The information is always available.

Whether the player acts upon it is their responsibility.

Example:

```
Bladder

Urgent
```

The player continues exploring for several more hours.

Any resulting consequences belong to that Chronicle.

---

# Future Expansion

The Body HUD is intentionally extensible.

Potential future additions include:

- Sleep Quality
- Pain
- Stress
- Mental State
- Disease Progression
- Addiction
- Pregnancy
- Permanent Disabilities
- Physical Adaptations

Any future category must satisfy one requirement:

> It represents information the body naturally perceives but the player cannot physically experience.

---

# Final Principle

The Body HUD is not a gameplay mechanic.

It is the Chronicle's nervous system translated into information the player can perceive.

The world remains mysterious.

The body remains truthful.

The choices remain entirely the player's.
