# 18.2 – Body HUD

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *The body remembers what the mind ignores.*

---

# Purpose

The Body HUD represents the physical awareness of the player's Chronicle.

In reality, humans receive constant information from their own bodies:

- hunger
- thirst
- fatigue
- pain
- temperature
- illness
- injury

In a text-based simulation, the player cannot physically experience these sensations.

The Body HUD exists to bridge this gap.

It provides the player with accurate awareness of their character's current physical state.

---

# Design Philosophy

The Body HUD follows one principle:

> **The HUD informs. The player decides.**

The system provides truthful information.

It does not:

- give advice
- suggest actions
- recommend solutions
- prevent mistakes
- protect players from consequences

The player's survival depends on how they interpret and respond to information.

# Body Information Categories

## Health

Represents the overall physical state of the Chronicle.

Examples:

```
Healthy

Injured

Wounded

Critical

Dying
```

Health is influenced by:

- injuries
- illness
- blood loss
- poisoning
- environmental damage
- untreated conditions

---

# Condition

Represents specific physical abnormalities affecting the Chronicle.

Examples:

```
Minor Cut

Bruised Rib

Broken Arm

Food Poisoning

Fever

Infection

Hypothermia

Venomous Bite
```

Multiple conditions may exist simultaneously.

Example:

```
Condition

Broken Arm
Infected Wound
High Fever
```

---

# Hunger

Represents the body's nutritional state.

Examples:

```
Full

Satisfied

Hungry

Very Hungry

Starving
```

Hunger does not directly kill instantly.

Instead, prolonged hunger affects:

- energy
- recovery
- physical capability
- decision making

---

# Thirst

Represents hydration level.

Examples:

```
Hydrated

Thirsty

Very Thirsty

Dehydrated

Severely Dehydrated
```

Water availability is one of the most important survival considerations.

---

# Energy

Represents physical exhaustion.

Examples:

```
Energetic

Rested

Tired

Fatigued

Exhausted

Collapsing
```

Energy affects:

- physical actions
- movement
- crafting efficiency
- combat capability
- recovery

---

# Temperature

Represents body temperature.

Examples:

```
Cold

Comfortable

Warm

Hot

Hypothermic

Hyperthermic
```

Body temperature is affected by:

- weather
- clothing
- shelter
- fire
- water exposure
- environment

---

# Condition Detail and Knowledge

The existence of a problem is always available to the player.

However, understanding depends on knowledge.

A Chronicle without medical knowledge may see:

```
Condition

Unknown Fever
```

A trained healer may see:

```
Condition

Respiratory Infection
```

A master physician may see:

```
Condition

Severe respiratory infection.
Elevated fever.
High risk of complications without treatment.
```

The body is the same.

Only understanding changes.

---

# No Numerical Values

The Body HUD intentionally avoids:

- hit points
- percentages
- meters
- numerical survival values

Examples:

Incorrect:

```
Health: 72%
Hunger: 45%
Temperature: -5
```

Correct:

```
Health: Injured
Hunger: Hungry
Temperature: Cold
```

The goal is human interpretation, not statistical optimization.

---

# Narrator Relationship

The narrator does not repeat Body HUD information.

Incorrect:

HUD:

```
Hunger: Starving
```

Narrator:

"You are starving."

Correct:

HUD:

```
Hunger: Starving
```

Narrator:

"The axe feels heavier with every swing."

---

The narrator describes consequences.

The Body HUD provides awareness.

---

# Player Responsibility

Project Draugr does not protect players from ignored conditions.

Example:

The player sees:

```
Thirst

Severely Dehydrated
```

The player chooses not to seek water.

The resulting consequences belong to that Chronicle.

The system provided the information.

The player made the decision.

---

# Future Expansion

The Body HUD may expand with additional biological systems:

Possible future categories:

- Sleep Quality
- Pain Level
- Mental State
- Stress
- Disease Progression
- Addiction
- Physical Adaptation
- Permanent Injuries

Any expansion must follow the same principle:

The HUD reveals what the character can perceive.

It does not replace experience.

---

# Final Principle

The Body HUD exists because the player cannot physically inhabit the body of their Chronicle.

It is not a game mechanic.

It is a replacement for human sensation.

The world remains mysterious.

The body remains truthful.

The player remains responsible.
