# 02 – World Foundation

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *The world existed long before humanity arrived.*

---

# Purpose

This document defines the permanent foundations of the Project Draugr universe.

It describes what exists within the world itself before any player arrives.

These foundations should remain stable throughout the lifetime of the project.

The world's state may evolve.

Its foundations should not.

---

# The World

Project Draugr takes place within a single persistent world existing parallel to Earth.

This world is neither a dream nor an alternate timeline.

It is an independent reality.

Its history began long before the arrival of humanity.

Its future continues regardless of human existence.

---

# Humanity

There is no living human civilization.

When the first Chronicle begins, the world contains:

- no villages
- no towns
- no cities
- no kingdoms
- no governments
- no merchants
- no blacksmiths
- no human societies

Every human settlement originates from transported humans.

Human civilization is entirely emergent.

---

# Geography

The world consists of permanently generated geography.

Examples include:

- Continents
- Regions
- Mountain ranges
- Valleys
- Plains
- Rivers
- Streams
- Lakes
- Oceans
- Forests
- Swamps
- Deserts
- Islands
- Coastlines

These geographical features never relocate.

Only their environmental conditions evolve.

---

# Regions

The world is divided into regions.

Each region possesses its own:

- climate
- ecology
- resources
- wildlife
- monster populations
- landmarks
- environmental hazards

Regions remain geographically fixed.

No two regions are identical.

---

# Ecosystems

Every region contains functioning ecosystems.

Examples include:

- forests
- wetlands
- rivers
- alpine environments
- grasslands
- caves
- coastal ecosystems

Each ecosystem contains unique life adapted to its environment.

---

# Resources

Natural resources exist independently of players.

Examples:

Wood

Stone

Clay

Ore

Coal

Water

Plants

Medicinal herbs

Fiber

Animals

Fish

Wild fruits

Mushrooms

These resources naturally regenerate according to ecological rules.

---

# Wildlife

Wildlife belongs to the ecosystem.

Animals are not enemies.

They are not allies.

They simply exist.

Examples:

- deer
- rabbits
- boars
- wolves
- bears
- birds
- reptiles
- insects
- aquatic life

Wildlife behaves according to survival instincts.

---

# Monsters

Monsters are natural inhabitants of the world.

They are not bosses.

They are not quest objectives.

They occupy ecological niches alongside ordinary wildlife.

Different regions contain different monster populations.

Some monsters are territorial.

Some migrate.

Some hunt.

Some remain dormant.

They are treated as another form of existence within the ecosystem.

---

# Ancient Civilization

Although humanity is absent, intelligent civilizations once existed.

Evidence survives through:

- ruins
- abandoned cities
- collapsed temples
- underground complexes
- relics
- forgotten roads
- mysterious artifacts

The fate of these civilizations remains unknown.

Project Draugr intentionally avoids confirming their disappearance.

Their ruins become part of exploration rather than exposition.

---

# Dungeons

Dungeons are remnants of forgotten ages.

They are physical locations within the world.

They are not instances.

They are not generated for individual players.

Every dungeon permanently exists within the world.

Its condition may evolve over time.

---

# Dangerous Areas

Certain regions naturally present elevated danger.

Examples include:

- corrupted forests
- deep caves
- volcanic regions
- cursed ruins
- monster territories
- unstable terrain

Danger arises from the world itself rather than scripted encounters.

---

# Climate

Each region possesses a stable climate.

Examples:

- temperate
- tropical
- alpine
- coastal
- arid
- wetlands

Climate influences ecology, wildlife, vegetation, and player survival.

---

# Weather

Weather exists independently of players.

Examples include:

- sunshine
- rainfall
- storms
- wind
- fog
- snowfall

Weather belongs to the simulation rather than scripted events.

---

# Time

Project Draugr follows real-world time.

The world calendar mirrors reality.

Days continue regardless of player presence.

Night falls even if nobody is watching.

---

# Human Civilization

Everything built by humanity becomes part of the world.

Examples:

- camps
- farms
- bridges
- roads
- villages
- towns
- cities
- kingdoms

Civilizations may expand.

Civilizations may disappear.

Their history remains part of the world.

---

# Invisible Reality

Not every existence belongs to the physical world.

Beyond ordinary perception exist two primordial principles.

## Creation

Creation expands existence.

It is responsible for continual emergence and renewal.

---

## Destruction

Destruction transforms existence.

It prevents stagnation through decay and change.

---

## Existence

Existence itself emerges only because Creation and Destruction remain eternally balanced.

Neither principle may completely dominate.

Players are unaware of these principles.

They perceive only their consequences.

---

# The Overseer

The Overseer exists beyond the world.

It never intervenes.

It never alters reality.

It remembers.

Every Chronicle.

Every discovery.

Every civilization.

Every death.

The Overseer preserves history.

Nothing more.

---

# Design Rules

The foundations described in this document are considered immutable.

Future updates may expand the world.

They should never contradict these principles.

When uncertainty arises during development, one question should always be asked:

> **Would this still exist if no players were present?**

If the answer is yes, it belongs in the world.

If the answer is no, it probably belongs somewhere else.
