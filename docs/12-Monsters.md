# 12 – Monsters

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *Not every predator resembles the animals of Earth.*

---

# Purpose

This document defines the monster system of Project Draugr.

Monsters are naturally occurring lifeforms native to the world.

They occupy ecological roles alongside wildlife while often possessing biological characteristics unfamiliar to transported humans.

Monsters are not manifestations of evil.

They are living organisms.

---

# Design Philosophy

Monsters are part of nature.

They are not dungeon bosses.

They are not scripted encounters.

They are not spawned for combat.

They exist because they evolved within this world.

Players fear them not because they are evil—

but because humanity does not yet understand them.

---

# Definition

A monster is any naturally occurring organism that differs significantly from Earth's known wildlife.

Differences may include:

- physiology
- intelligence
- behavior
- adaptation
- size
- environmental specialization

The distinction between wildlife and monsters is descriptive rather than moral.

---

# Ecological Role

Every monster species occupies a niche within the ecosystem.

Possible roles include:

- apex predator
- ambush predator
- scavenger
- parasite
- territorial guardian
- opportunistic hunter
- decomposer

No monster exists solely to fight players.

---

# Classification

Monster species may be categorized according to ecological function.

---

## Predators

Primary hunters of other organisms.

Examples:

- pursuit hunters
- ambush hunters
- pack hunters

---

## Territorial Species

Creatures aggressively defending a defined habitat.

Conflict usually occurs only after intrusion.

---

## Scavengers

Species feeding upon remains of dead organisms.

Often avoid unnecessary conflict.

---

## Parasitic Species

Creatures surviving by exploiting larger organisms.

Their danger often comes from subtlety rather than direct combat.

---

## Environmental Specialists

Species adapted to extreme environments.

Examples include:

- volcanic regions
- deep caves
- swamps
- frozen mountains

---

# Behavior

Monster behavior follows survival principles.

They naturally:

- hunt
- rest
- drink
- defend territory
- reproduce
- migrate
- compete
- avoid unnecessary injury

Combat is costly.

Most monsters avoid risk whenever possible.

---

# Intelligence

Different species possess different levels of intelligence.

Possible capabilities include:

- instinct
- learned behavior
- coordinated hunting
- problem solving
- memory
- environmental adaptation

Intelligence is biological, not magical.

---

# Fear

Not every monster attacks humans.

Some avoid them.

Some ignore them.

Some investigate them.

Some hunt them.

Behavior depends upon the species and situation.

---

# Territory

Many monsters establish territories.

Territories influence:

- encounters
- hunting patterns
- reproduction
- competition

Territories naturally shift over time.

---

# Population

Monster populations fluctuate naturally.

Population changes result from:

- births
- deaths
- food availability
- competition
- environmental conditions

Monsters are subject to ecological limits.

---

# Reproduction

Monsters reproduce according to species.

Different reproductive strategies may exist.

Examples include:

- live birth
- egg laying
- seasonal breeding
- colony reproduction

Reproduction is part of natural ecology.

---

# Mortality

Monsters die.

Causes include:

- starvation
- combat
- predation
- disease
- age
- environmental hazards

No species is immortal.

---

# Human Interaction

Transported humans gradually become part of monster ecology.

Possible outcomes include:

Humans becoming prey.

Humans becoming competitors.

Humans altering territories.

Humans driving migration.

Monsters adapt to persistent human activity.

---

# Knowledge

Players initially know nothing about monster species.

Knowledge must be earned through:

Observation.

Tracking.

Experimentation.

Survival.

Failure.

Every encounter contributes to understanding.

---

# Discovery

New monster species should feel significant.

Discoveries may reveal:

- habitat
- diet
- behavior
- weaknesses
- seasonal activity
- territorial boundaries

Knowledge often proves more valuable than combat.

---

# Resources

Defeated monsters may provide useful materials.

Examples include:

- hides
- bones
- horns
- scales
- claws
- teeth
- specialized organs

These are consequences of interacting with the ecosystem—not rewards.

---

# Adaptation

Monster populations gradually respond to environmental changes.

Examples include:

Avoiding settlements.

Changing migration routes.

Competing for shrinking habitats.

Expanding into abandoned regions.

Nature continually adapts.

---

# Rare Species

Some monsters exist in extremely low populations.

Rare species should remain naturally uncommon.

Their rarity increases the value of discovery rather than merely increasing combat difficulty.

---

# Apex Species

Some ecosystems contain apex monsters.

These creatures significantly influence surrounding ecological balance.

Removing one may permanently alter the ecosystem.

Their existence should matter beyond combat.

---

# Extinction

Regional extinction is possible.

Global extinction should remain exceptionally rare.

Removing an entire species permanently changes the world's ecological history.

Such consequences should emerge naturally rather than through scripted events.

---

# Monster Records

Every encountered species becomes part of the player's Chronicle.

Entries may include:

- appearance
- habitat
- behavior
- ecological role
- observed abilities
- danger assessment
- collected resources
- personal observations

Incomplete records gradually become more accurate through repeated encounters.

---

# Design Principles

Monster systems should satisfy the following principles.

## Biological

Monsters are living organisms.

---

## Ecological

Every species belongs somewhere.

---

## Independent

Monsters exist regardless of player presence.

---

## Adaptive

Species respond naturally to changing conditions.

---

## Persistent

Populations continue evolving while players are offline.

---

## Mysterious

Players should never immediately understand every species.

Curiosity should be preserved.

---

# Final Principle

Monsters are not villains.

They are not obstacles placed before heroes.

They are ancient inhabitants of a world that never belonged to humanity.

Some may fear humans.

Some may hunt them.

Some may never acknowledge their existence.

To the monsters, transported humans are simply another unfamiliar creature entering an already living world.
