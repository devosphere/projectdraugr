# Purpose

This document defines how individuals gain, lose, and maintain reputation within the world.

It establishes:

- recognition;
- reputation;
- fame;
- infamy;
- titles;
- historical identity.

---

# Design Philosophy

A person's impact is measured not only by what they did.

It is measured by how the world remembers what they did.

---

# Core Principle

## Reputation Is The Shadow Cast By Actions Across Time.

---

# Reputation Definition

Reputation is the collective perception of an individual.

It is influenced by:

```
Actions

+

Witnesses

+

Records

+

Stories

+

Interpretation
```

---

# Reputation Is Not Absolute

Different groups may view the same person differently.

---

# Example

A conqueror:

Civilization A:

"Great liberator."

Civilization B:

"Brutal invader."

---

# Reputation Layers

Reputation exists at multiple levels.

```
Personal

↓

Local

↓

Regional

↓

Civilizational

↓

Historical
```

---

# Personal Reputation

How individuals perceive someone.

Influenced by:

- direct interactions;
- memories;
- relationships.

---

# Local Reputation

Recognition within:

- villages;
- towns;
- communities.

---

# Regional Reputation

Recognition across:

- multiple settlements;
- territories.

---

# Civilization Reputation

Recognition across:

- nations;
- cultures;
- political systems.

---

# Historical Reputation

Recognition across generations.

---

# Reputation Sources

Reputation comes from:

---

# Direct Actions

Examples:

- helping people;
- causing harm;
- completing achievements.

---

# Witness Accounts

People spread stories about what they see.

---

# Records

Examples:

- books;
- monuments;
- official documents.

---

# Rumors

Information may spread without verification.

---

# Reputation Accuracy

Reputation may be:

```
True

+

Exaggerated

+

Incomplete

+

False
```

---

# Fame System

Fame represents positive recognition.

---

# Fame Sources

Examples:

- heroic actions;
- discoveries;
- leadership;
- achievements.

---

# Fame Effects

Possible results:

- people seek assistance;
- invitations appear;
- opportunities increase.

---

# Fame Limitations

Fame does not guarantee:

- respect;
- trust;
- authority.

---

# Infamy System

Infamy represents negative recognition.

---

# Infamy Sources

Examples:

- crimes;
- betrayal;
- destruction;
- cruelty.

---

# Infamy Effects

Possible results:

- fear;
- hostility;
- avoidance.

---

# Reputation Evolution

Reputation changes over time.

Factors:

- new actions;
- changing opinions;
- historical reinterpretation.

---

# Reputation Decay

Without continued relevance:

Recognition may fade.

---

# Example

A famous explorer:

Generation One:

"Legendary discoverer."

Generation Five:

"Obscure historical figure."

---

# Reputation Preservation

Important individuals may be remembered through:

- statues;
- books;
- traditions;
- descendants.

---

- The Founder.
- The Wanderer.
- The Betrayer.
- The Last Guardian.

---

# Title Creation

Titles emerge from:

- actions;

# Title Evolution

A title may change meaning.

A feared enemy.

Later meaning:

A warrior who destroyed an ancient threat.

# Final Statement

A person's life ends.

Their actions remain.

A name can disappear.

A story can survive.

A legend can outlive the person who created it.

The Awakened Soul is not remembered because they exist.

They are remembered because the world changes because they existed.
