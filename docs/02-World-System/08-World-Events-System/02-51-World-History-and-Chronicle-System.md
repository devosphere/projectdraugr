# Purpose

This document defines how the world records, preserves, and interprets history.

It establishes:

- historical timelines;
- eras;
- chronicles;
- world memory;
- legends;
- player impact preservation.

---

# Design Philosophy

History is not just what happened.

History is what survives.

---

# Core Principle

## Events Become History When Their Impact Outlives The Moment.

---

# History Definition

History is the accumulated record of:

- events;
- civilizations;
- individuals;
- discoveries;
- conflicts;
- transformations.

---

# Historical Layers

History exists at multiple levels.

```
Personal History

↓

Family History

↓

Community History

↓

Civilization History

↓

World History
```

---

# Personal History

The memories of individuals.

Examples:

- experiences;
- relationships;
- achievements.

---

# Family History

Stories passed between generations.

Examples:

- ancestors;
- traditions;
- inherited items.

---

# Community History

Shared local memories.

Examples:

- settlement founders;
- disasters;
- important events.

---

# Civilization History

Large-scale records.

Examples:

- wars;
- rulers;
- discoveries;
- cultural changes.

---

# World History

Events affecting the greater universe.

Examples:

- world-changing disasters;
- major discoveries;
- existence-level events.

---

# Chronicle Definition

A Chronicle is a recorded sequence of meaningful events.

A Chronicle may belong to:

- an individual;
- civilization;
- Awakened Soul;
- world era.

---

# Chronicle Purpose

Chronicles preserve:

- actions;
- consequences;
- transformations.

---

# World Timeline

The world follows continuous time.

---

# Time Structure


---

# Historical Events

Not every event becomes history.

Events become historical when they create lasting impact.

---

# Historical Importance Factors

Determined by:

- scale;
- consequences;
- uniqueness;
- cultural impact.

---

# Example

A person helping one traveler:

May become personal history.

A person saving a civilization:

May become world history.

---

# Era System

---

Examples:

- rise of civilization;
- technological revolution;
- catastrophic event.

---

# Era Examples

```

```
