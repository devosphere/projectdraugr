# Purpose

This document defines how mysteries, unexplained phenomena, and unknown aspects of the universe exist within Project Draugr.

It establishes:

- mysteries;
- unknown phenomena;
- incomplete knowledge;
- hidden truths;
- unexplained events;
- discovery limitations.

---

# Design Philosophy

A universe without mystery feels artificial.

---

# Core Principle

## The Unknown Is Not A Missing Feature. The Unknown Is Part Of The World.

---

# Mystery Definition

A mystery is something that exists but is not completely understood.

A mystery may involve:

- history;
- existence;
- technology;
- life;
- reality;
- the universe itself.

---

# Mystery Categories

```
Historical Mysteries

+

Environmental Mysteries

+

Scientific Mysteries

+

Existential Mysteries

+

Overseer Mysteries

+

Unknown Phenomena
```

---

# Historical Mysteries

Questions about the past.

Examples:

- Why did an ancient civilization disappear?
- Who created a forgotten structure?
- What happened during a lost era?

---

# Environmental Mysteries

Unknown natural phenomena.

Examples:

- strange regions;
- unusual weather;
- impossible geological formations.

---

# Scientific Mysteries

Phenomena not yet understood.

Examples:

- unknown materials;
- unexplained energy;
- strange biological behavior.

---

# Existential Mysteries

Questions about existence.

Examples:

- origin of life;
- purpose of consciousness;
- nature of reality.

---

# Overseer Mysteries

Events connected to the greater structure of the universe.

Examples:

- why worlds exist;
- why Awakened Souls are chosen;
- the purpose of Chronicles.

---

# Unknown Phenomena

Events that cannot immediately be explained.

Examples:

- strange lights;
- impossible structures;
- unusual creatures;
- unexplained changes.

---

# Mystery Design Principles

Mysteries should have:

## Existence

The mystery must actually exist.

---

## Evidence

There must be clues.

---

## Interpretation

Different groups may understand it differently.

---

## Consequence

Discovering information should matter.

---

# Knowledge Limitation System

No entity knows everything.

Knowledge depends on:

```
Experience

+

Culture

+

Technology

+

Location

+

History
```

---

# Civilization Understanding

Civilizations create explanations based on their knowledge.

---

# Example

A mysterious object appears.

Civilization A:

"Ancient divine artifact."

Civilization B:

"Unknown technology."

Civilization C:

"Natural phenomenon."

---

# Truth Versus Belief

The world contains:

```
Actual Reality

+

Perceived Reality
```

---

# Example

A creature exists.

Reality:

Ancient biological species.

Belief:

Guardian spirit.

---

# Mystery Discovery

Mysteries are uncovered through:

- exploration;
- research;
- conversations;
- artifacts;
- events.

---

# Mystery Progression

A mystery develops through:

```
Observation

↓

Clues

↓

Theories

↓

Partial Understanding

↓

Possible Truth
```

---

# Partial Truth System

Discoveries may reveal only fragments.

---

# Example

Player discovers:

"The civilization vanished after entering the underground city."

Unknown:

"Why did they enter?"

---

# Multiple Interpretations

Not every mystery has one accepted answer.

---

# Example

A ruined temple may represent:

Theory A:

Religious purpose.

Theory B:

Scientific research facility.

Theory C:

Combination of both.

---

# Forbidden Knowledge

Some knowledge may be dangerous.

Examples:

- destructive technology;
- ancient secrets;
- dangerous information.

---

# Knowledge Consequences

Discovering information may:

- advance civilization;
- create conflict;
- change beliefs.

---

# Mystery Preservation

Some mysteries should remain unsolved.

Reasons:

- scale beyond understanding;
- incomplete evidence;
- lost information.

---

# Unknown Does Not Mean Random

Mysteries must follow world logic.

---

# Example

A strange phenomenon should have:

- rules;
- consistency;
- history.

---

# Player Interaction

The Awakened Soul may:

- investigate;
- ignore;
- reveal;
- hide information.

---

# Player Reputation Impact

Revealing discoveries may create:

- fame;
- distrust;
- conflict.

---

# Civilization Reaction

Different societies react differently.

Possible reactions:

- acceptance;
- fear;
- worship;
- denial;
- exploitation.

---

# Mystery Evolution

Mysteries change as knowledge grows.

---

# Example

Ancient myth:

"The stars are watching us."

Later discovery:

An advanced civilization existed beyond the world.

---

# World Continuity

Mysteries continue evolving while players are absent.

---

# Example

A strange phenomenon appears.

Years later:

- researchers studied it;
- theories changed;
- civilizations reacted.

# Final Statement

A living universe is not only made of answers.

It is made of questions.

The greatest explorers are not those who know everything.

They are those who continue searching despite knowing they may never understand.

The Awakened Soul enters a world filled with forgotten histories, hidden truths, and impossible questions.

The unknown is not outside the world.

The unknown is part of it.
