# Purpose

This document defines large-scale destructive events and how the world responds to them.

It establishes:

- disasters;
- catastrophes;
- survival responses;
- aftermath;
- rebuilding;
- historical consequences.

---

# Design Philosophy

Destruction is part of creation.

---

# Core Principle

## A Catastrophe Does Not End The World. It Changes The World.

---

# Disaster Definition

A disaster is a major event capable of significantly affecting:

- populations;
- ecosystems;
- settlements;
- civilizations;
- history.

---

# Disaster Categories

```
Natural Disasters

+

Biological Disasters

+

Environmental Disasters

+

Civilization Disasters

+

Unknown Catastrophes
```

---

# Natural Disasters

Events caused by planetary forces.

Examples:

- earthquakes;
- volcanic eruptions;
- tsunamis;
- storms;
- extreme weather.

---

# Earthquake System

Earthquakes may affect:

- terrain;
- buildings;
- roads;
- underground structures.

---

# Earthquake Consequences

Possible outcomes:

- destroyed settlements;
- damaged infrastructure;
- displaced populations;
- new geological formations.

---

# Volcanic Activity

Volcanoes may:

- destroy regions;
- create new land;
- change ecosystems.

---

# Volcanic Aftermath

Possible changes:

- fertile soil;
- new resources;
- dangerous environments.

---

# Tsunami System

Large water events may affect:

- coastal settlements;
- trade routes;
- ecosystems.

---

# Storm Catastrophes

Extreme storms may create:

- flooding;
- destruction;
- isolation.

---

# Biological Disasters

Events involving living organisms.

Examples:

- diseases;
- invasive species;
- ecosystem collapse.

---

# Disease Outbreak System

Diseases may spread through:

- population density;
- trade routes;
- environmental conditions.

---

# Disease Response

Civilizations may:

- quarantine;
- develop treatments;
- migrate;
- suffer decline.

---

# Environmental Disasters

Long-term environmental damage.

Examples:

- drought;
- desertification;
- resource collapse.

---

# Climate Shift

Large environmental changes may affect:

- agriculture;
- migration;
- civilization stability.

---

# Civilization Disasters

Catastrophes caused by intelligent societies.

Examples:

- wars;
- economic collapse;
- political collapse.

---

# War Catastrophe

Large conflicts may cause:

- population decline;
- destroyed infrastructure;
- cultural loss.

---

# Civilization Collapse

A civilization may collapse when:

```
Resources Decline

+

Political Instability

+

External Pressure

+

Internal Failure
```

---

# Unknown Catastrophes

Events beyond common understanding.

Examples:

- ancient mechanisms;
- unexplained phenomena;
- world-changing discoveries.

---

# Disaster Severity Levels

Disasters are classified by impact.

---

# Level 1 - Local Disaster

Affected Area:

Small region.

Examples:

- village flood;
- building collapse.

---

# Level 2 - Regional Disaster

Affected Area:

Multiple settlements.

Examples:

- major drought;
- regional plague.

---

# Level 3 - Civilization Disaster

Affected Area:

Entire civilizations.

Examples:

- empire collapse;
- massive war.

---

# Level 4 - World Changing Event

Affected Area:

Multiple regions.

Examples:

- planetary environmental changes.

---

# Disaster Warning System

Events may have warning periods.

Examples:

- weather patterns;
- scientific observation;
- rumors;
- natural signs.

---

# Warning Accuracy

Depends on:

- technology;
- knowledge;
- culture.

---

# Example

Advanced civilization:

Predicts volcanic activity.

Primitive civilization:

Interprets signs through tradition.

---

# Disaster Preparation

Civilizations may prepare through:

- evacuation plans;
- stored resources;
- construction;
- knowledge.

---

# Preparation Effects

Prepared societies have:

- lower casualties;
- faster recovery.

---

# Unprepared Societies

May experience:

- population loss;
- migration;
- collapse.

---

# Player Interaction

- warn people;

- ignore events.

# Player Cannot Stop Everything
