# Purpose

This document defines how discoveries and exploration events occur within the world.

It establishes:

- exploration opportunities;
- discoveries;
- mysteries;
- ancient locations;
- knowledge advancement;
- exploration consequences.

---

# Design Philosophy

The universe is vast.

Not everything has been found.

---

# Core Principle

## Discovery Is Not Created By The Player. It Is Revealed By The Player.

---

# Discovery Definition

A discovery is something that exists in the world but is unknown to certain individuals or civilizations.

Examples:

- locations;
- creatures;
- resources;
- technologies;
- historical information.

---

# Exploration Model

Exploration follows:

```
Unknown Area

↓

Observation

↓

Investigation

↓

Discovery

↓

Knowledge Spread

↓

World Reaction
```

---

# Unknown Regions

The world contains areas not fully understood.

Reasons:

- distance;
- danger;
- lack of technology;
- cultural barriers.

---

# Discovery Conditions

Discoveries may depend on:

- location;
- time;
- player actions;
- civilization knowledge.

---

# Example

An ancient ruin may only be discovered when:

- a storm reveals an entrance;
- a map is found;
- an explorer investigates.

---

# Discovery Rarity

Discoveries have rarity levels.

# Common Discoveries

---

# Rare Discoveries

Examples:

# Legendary Discoveries

Examples:

# Discovery Ownership

# Knowledge Spread Speed

# Final Statement

The greatest moments in a living universe are not always victories.

Sometimes they are moments of realization.

Standing before something nobody has seen.

Reading a story written thousands of years ago.

Finding evidence that the world is much larger than expected.

The Awakened Soul does not create the unknown.

They are simply brave enough to seek it.
