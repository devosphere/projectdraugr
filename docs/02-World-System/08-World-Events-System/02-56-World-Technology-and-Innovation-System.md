# Purpose

This document defines how technology develops, spreads, evolves, and disappears within the world.

It establishes:

- innovation;
- research;
- technological advancement;
- knowledge progression;
- technological adaptation;
- technological decline.

---

# Design Philosophy

Technology is a response to the needs of life.

---

# Core Principle

## Civilizations Do Not Advance Because They Can. They Advance Because They Must.

---

# Technology Definition

Technology represents applied knowledge used to:

- solve problems;
- improve efficiency;
- create possibilities.

---

# Technology Categories

```
Survival Technology

+

Agricultural Technology

+

Crafting Technology

+

Engineering Technology

+

Scientific Technology

+

Advanced Technology
```

---

# Survival Technology

Technology supporting basic existence.

Examples:

- tools;
- shelters;
- fire usage;
- clothing.

---

# Agricultural Technology

Technology improving food production.

Examples:

- farming methods;
- irrigation;
- animal domestication.

---

# Crafting Technology

Technology involving production.

Examples:

- metallurgy;
- manufacturing;
- construction methods.

---

# Engineering Technology

Technology involving large structures.

Examples:

- bridges;
- machines;
- infrastructure.

---

# Scientific Technology

Technology based on deeper understanding.

Examples:

- medicine;
- energy;
- research systems.

---

# Advanced Technology

Technology beyond current common knowledge.

Examples:

- highly developed systems;
- ancient lost technologies;
- civilization-level breakthroughs.

---

# Innovation System

Innovation occurs when knowledge creates new solutions.

---

# Innovation Sources

Innovation comes from:

```
Necessity

+

Curiosity

+

Competition

+

Discovery

+

Crisis
```

---

# Necessity-Driven Innovation

Problems create solutions.

Example:

Drought creates:

- improved irrigation;
- water storage.

---

# Curiosity-Driven Innovation

Exploration creates knowledge.

Example:

Studying stars creates navigation improvements.

---

# Competition-Driven Innovation

Rival civilizations accelerate development.

Examples:

- military technology;
- economic improvements.

---

# Crisis-Driven Innovation

Disasters force adaptation.

Examples:

- stronger buildings;
- medical advancement.

---
