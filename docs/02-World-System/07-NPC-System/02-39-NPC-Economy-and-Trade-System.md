# Purpose

This document defines how NPC economies function within Project Draugr.

It establishes:

- production;
- consumption;
- trade;
- resource flow;
- economic behavior;
- market changes.

---

# Design Philosophy

Economy is the movement of survival, resources, and human effort.

---

# Core Principle

## Value Exists Because Someone Needs Something.

Items do not have fixed importance.

Their value changes based on circumstances.

---

# Economy Structure

The economy operates through:

```
Resources

↓

Production

↓

Distribution

↓

Exchange

↓

Consumption
```

---

# Resources

Resources are the foundation of economic activity.

Examples:

- food;
- wood;
- stone;
- metals;
- cloth;
- tools;
- knowledge.

---

# Resource Availability

Resources depend on:

- geography;
- climate;
- population;
- technology;
- environmental conditions.

---

# Production

NPCs transform resources into useful goods.

Examples:

Farmers:

Produce food.

---

Miners:

Extract materials.

---

Craftsmen:

Create tools and equipment.

---

Merchants:

Move goods between locations.

---

# Specialization

Societies develop specialization.

Reasons:

- efficiency;
- available resources;
- cultural preference;
- skill development.

---

# Production Chains

Complex items require multiple contributors.

---

# Example: Steel Weapon

Requires:

```
Mining

↓

Ore Processing

↓

Metal Refining

↓

Forging

↓

Trading

↓

Usage
```

---

# Economic Dependency

No individual is fully independent.

Society functions through cooperation.

---

# NPC Consumption

NPCs require resources to live.

Examples:

Food:

Needed for survival.

Tools:

Needed for productivity.

Shelter:

Needed for protection.

---

# Supply And Demand

Economic value changes based on:

- availability;
- demand;
- urgency;
- difficulty.

---

# Example

During winter:

Food becomes more valuable.

During peace:

Weapons may decrease in demand.

During war:

Weapons become valuable.

---

# Currency System

Civilizations may develop different methods of exchange.

Examples:

- barter;
- coins;
- trade goods;
- reputation-based exchange.

---

# Currency Development

Currency depends on:

- civilization maturity;
- culture;
- technology;
- resources.

---

# Trade System

Trade connects communities.

---

# Local Trade

Within settlements.

Examples:

- farmers selling crops;
- craftsmen selling tools.

---

# Regional Trade

Between settlements.

Examples:

- food exchange;
- material transport.

---

# Long-Distance Trade

Between civilizations.

Examples:

- rare resources;
- cultural goods;
- advanced technology.

---

# Merchant Behavior

Merchants evaluate:

- profit;
- safety;
- distance;
- demand.

---

# Trade Routes

Trade routes emerge naturally.

Influenced by:

- geography;
- security;
- resources;
- political relationships.

---

# Trade Route Changes

Routes may change due to:

- conflict;
- disasters;
- discoveries;
- player actions.

---

# Economic Growth

A settlement grows economically through:

- stable production;
- population;
- trade connections;
- innovation.

---

# Economic Decline

A settlement may suffer from:

- resource shortage;
- isolation;
- conflict;
- environmental damage.

---

# Scarcity System

Scarcity creates value.

Causes:

- limited resources;
- disasters;
- increased demand;
- population growth.

---

# Surplus System

Excess resources may:

- lower value;
- encourage trade;
- support expansion.

---

# Player Economic Influence

The player may affect economies.

Examples:

Creating:

- farms;
- workshops;
- trade routes;
- discoveries.

---

# Player Disruption

The player may also harm economies.

Examples:

- destroying resources;
- monopolizing goods;
- disrupting trade.

---

# Economic Reputation

NPCs may remember economic behavior.

Examples:

A fair trader gains trust.

A dishonest trader gains suspicion.

---

# NPC Economic Decisions

NPCs consider:

```
Need

+

Available Resources

+

Risk

+

Personal Goals

+

Social Conditions
```
