# Purpose

This document defines how NPCs participate in societies and maintain daily lives.

It establishes:

- social structures;
- family systems;
- occupations;
- responsibilities;
- community interactions;
- daily routines.

---

# Design Philosophy

Civilizations are not created by buildings.

They are created by people.

---

# Core Principle

## Society Emerges From Individual Lives.

A settlement exists because individuals contribute to it.

---

# NPC Social Structure

NPC society is organized through:

```
Individual

↓

Family

↓

Community

↓

Settlement

↓

Civilization
```

---

# Individual

The smallest social unit.

An NPC has:

- personal goals;
- needs;
- relationships;
- responsibilities.

---

# Family Structure

Families provide:

- emotional support;
- survival cooperation;
- cultural transfer.

---

# Family Relationships

Possible relationships:

- parent;
- child;
- sibling;
- partner;
- relative.

---

# Family Memory

Families preserve:

- ancestry;
- traditions;
- achievements;
- stories.

---

# Community Structure

Communities form around shared needs.

Examples:

- villages;
- towns;
- tribes;
- organizations.

---

# Community Functions

Communities provide:

- safety;
- cooperation;
- specialization;
- cultural identity.

---

# Social Roles

NPCs naturally develop roles.

Examples:

- farmer;
- hunter;
- builder;
- merchant;
- soldier;
- scholar;
- leader.

---

# Occupation System

Occupations are not fixed classes.

They develop through:

```
Ability

+

Interest

+

Opportunity

+

Social Need
```

---

# Occupation Change

NPCs may change professions.

Reasons:

- aging;
- opportunity;
- personal choice;
- economic changes.

---

# Example

A farmer may become:

- a merchant after discovering trade opportunities;
- a soldier during conflict;
- a teacher later in life.

---

# Daily Life Simulation

NPCs follow realistic schedules.

---

# Morning Activities

Examples:

- waking;
- eating;
- preparing tools;
- checking responsibilities.

---

# Work Period

Examples:

Farmers:

- tending crops;
- caring for animals.

---

Craftsmen:

- producing goods;
- repairing equipment.

---

Merchants:

- trading;
- traveling.

---

# Social Period

NPCs interact through:

- conversation;
- gatherings;
- entertainment;
- cultural activities.

---

# Rest Period

NPCs:

- return home;
- recover;
- spend time with family.

---

# Routine Variation

NPC schedules change due to:

- weather;
- emergencies;
- festivals;
- conflicts;
- personal events.

---

# Community Events

Societies create events.

Examples:

- celebrations;
- markets;
- ceremonies;
- meetings.

---

# Cultural Activities

Communities preserve identity through:

- traditions;
- stories;
- rituals;
- art.

---

# Economic Participation

NPCs contribute to the economy.

Examples:

Farmers produce food.

Craftsmen create tools.

Merchants distribute resources.

---

# Resource Flow

A settlement functions through:

```
Production

↓

Exchange

↓

Consumption

↓

Growth
```

---

# Social Dependency

NPCs depend on each other.

Examples:

A farmer needs:

- tools from craftsmen;
- protection from defenders.

A craftsman needs:

- materials from gatherers;
- food from farmers.

---

# Community Growth

Settlements grow when:

- resources are available;
- population increases;
- safety improves.

---

# Community Decline

Settlements may weaken through:

- resource shortages;
- disasters;
- conflict;
- disease;
- population loss.

---

# NPC Migration

NPCs may move.

Reasons:

- opportunity;
- danger;
- relationships;
- exploration.

---

# New Settlement Formation

NPC groups may create new settlements.

Conditions:

- available resources;
- leadership;
- motivation;
- safety.

---

# Leadership

Communities may develop leaders.

Leadership emerges from:

- reputation;
- ability;
- relationships;
- achievement.

---

# NPC Politics

Social decisions may involve:

- cooperation;

A world is not alive because it has cities.

A world is alive because people wake up inside those cities.

They work.

They love.
