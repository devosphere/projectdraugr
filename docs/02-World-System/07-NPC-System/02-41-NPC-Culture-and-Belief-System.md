# Purpose

This document defines how NPC cultures and beliefs develop, spread, and influence civilizations.

It establishes:

- traditions;
- values;
- beliefs;
- customs;
- languages;
- artistic expression;
- cultural evolution.

---

# Design Philosophy

A civilization is not only where people live.

It is how they understand life.

---

# Core Principle

## People Create Meaning From Their Experiences.

Culture is the memory of generations.

---

# Culture Definition

Culture represents the shared identity of a group.

It includes:

- beliefs;
- customs;
- traditions;
- language;
- art;
- values.

---

# Cultural Formation

Culture develops through:

```
Environment

+

History

+

Challenges

+

Discoveries

+

Collective Memory
```

---

# Environmental Influence

The world shapes culture.

Examples:

A desert civilization may value:

- water preservation;
- navigation;
- endurance.

A coastal civilization may value:

- sailing;
- trade;
- exploration.

---

# Historical Influence

Past events shape present beliefs.

Examples:

A civilization that survived a disaster may develop:

- strong preparation traditions;
- fear of repeating history.

---

# Cultural Values

Examples:

- loyalty;
- courage.

---

## Knowledge-Based Culture

Values:

- discovery;
- education;
- research.

---

## Survival-Based Culture

Values:

- preparation;
- resource management;
- resilience.

---

## Community-Based Culture

Values:

- cooperation;
- family;
- collective success.

# Belief Systems

Beliefs explain:

- existence;
- nature;
- unknown phenomena;
- death;
- the universe.

---

# Beliefs Are Not Always Religion

A belief system may include:

- philosophy;
- traditions;
- scientific understanding;
- spiritual practices.

---

# Religion And Spirituality

Some civilizations may develop religions.

Religion may include:

- deities;
- rituals;
- sacred places;
- moral teachings.

---

# Religious Development

Beliefs evolve from:

- natural events;
- historical figures;
- mysteries;
- discoveries.

---

# Example

A civilization sees a powerful natural phenomenon.

Possible interpretations:

Scientific:

"An unusual environmental event."

Spiritual:

"A message from higher beings."

---

# Cultural Diversity

Different civilizations may interpret the same world differently.

---

# Example

"Guardian of existence."

"Unknown force beyond understanding."

"Ancient machine."

# Cultural Evolution

Culture changes over time.

Influences:

- migration;
- technology;
- conflict;
- discoveries;
- player actions.

# Cultural Exchange

Civilizations influence each other.

Examples:

- borrowed technology;

# Cultural Conflict

Examples:

A traditional society may resist:

- foreign customs;
- new technologies;
- outside influence.
