# Purpose

This document defines the foundation of NPC existence and behavior within Project Draugr.

It establishes NPCs as independent simulated individuals with:

- personal identities;
- needs;
- memories;
- behaviors;
- relationships;
- life cycles.

---

# Design Philosophy

NPCs are not content.

NPCs are inhabitants.

---

# Core Principle

## The World Does Not Wait For The Player.

NPCs continue living regardless of whether the Awakened Soul is present.

---

# NPC Definition

An NPC is any intelligent non-player individual existing within the world.

Examples:

- villagers;
- merchants;
- craftsmen;
- leaders;
- travelers;
- researchers.

---

# NPC Life Structure

Every NPC follows:

```
Creation

↓

Childhood

↓

Learning

↓

Adulthood

↓

Contribution

↓

Aging

↓

Death

↓

Legacy
```

---

# NPC Identity

Every significant NPC has:

- name;
- appearance;
- species;
- origin;
- personality;
- occupation;
- memories.

---

# Individuality Principle

NPCs should not feel like copies.

Individuals should have differences in:

- behavior;
- preferences;
- decision making;
- relationships.

---

# NPC Needs

NPCs have survival requirements.

Examples:

- food;
- water;
- shelter;
- safety;
- social connection.

---

# NPC Motivation

NPC behavior is influenced by goals.

Examples:

A farmer may want:

- better crops;
- family security;
- land ownership.

A merchant may want:

- wealth;
- trade routes;
- reputation.

A researcher may want:

- knowledge;
- discoveries.

---

# NPC Daily Life

NPCs follow routines.

Examples:

Morning:

- wake;
- prepare;
- begin activities.

Day:

- work;
- travel;
- interact.

Night:

- rest;
- socialize;
- recover.

---

# Routine Adaptation

NPCs adjust routines based on:

- weather;
- danger;
- resources;
- social conditions.

---

# NPC Personality

Personality influences behavior.

Possible traits:

- cautious;
- brave;
- curious;
- ambitious;
- generous;
- selfish;
- patient.

---

# Personality Is Not A Restriction

Traits influence decisions.

They do not completely determine behavior.

---

# NPC Learning

NPCs can learn.

Sources:

- experience;
- teaching;
- observation;
- failure.

---

# NPC Skill Development

NPCs improve through:

- practice;
- mentorship;
- occupation.

---

# NPC Occupations

Occupations emerge from:

- abilities;
- interests;
- social needs.

Examples:

- farmer;
- blacksmith;
- builder;
- soldier;
- scholar;
- merchant.

---

# NPC Relationships

NPCs form connections.

Examples:

- family;
- friendship;
- rivalry;
- mentorship;
- partnership.

---

# Relationship Memory

NPCs remember meaningful interactions.

Examples:

An NPC remembers:

- who helped them;
- who harmed them;
- who betrayed them.

---

# NPC Emotions

NPCs experience emotional states.

Examples:

- happiness;
- fear;
- grief;
- anger;
- gratitude.

---

# Emotional Responses

Emotions influence:

- decisions;
- relationships;
- behavior.

---

# NPC Decision Making

NPC decisions consider:

```
Needs

+

Personality

+

Memory

+

Environment

+

Goals
```

---

# NPC Independence

NPCs may:

- travel;
- change jobs;
- create relationships;
- leave settlements;
- create new settlements.

---

# NPC Death

NPCs can die naturally.

Causes:

- age;
- illness;
- accidents;
- conflict;
- environment.

---

# NPC Legacy

NPCs may leave:

- descendants;
- knowledge;
- achievements;
- reputation.

---

# Generational Continuity

Families and communities may continue beyond individuals.

---

# NPC Reaction To Awakened Souls

NPCs do not automatically trust the player.

The Awakened Soul is:

- unknown;
- foreign;
- unusual.

---

# First Impression

NPC perception depends on:

- appearance;
- actions;
- communication;
- reputation.

---

# NPC Knowledge Limitations

NPCs do not know everything.

They only know:

- personal experiences;
- information received;
- cultural knowledge.

---

# Rumors And Information

NPCs may spread information.

However:

Information may be:

- incomplete;
- inaccurate;
- biased.

---

# NPC Simulation Priority

Not every NPC requires equal simulation detail.

---

# Important NPCs

Receive deeper simulation:

- memories;
- relationships;
- detailed behavior.

---

# Background NPCs

Receive simplified simulation:

- basic needs;
- routines;
- contribution.

---

# Edge Cases

The interaction may still become memorable.

---

## NPC Leaves Player Area

The NPC continues existing.

---

## Important NPC Dies

The world reacts.

# Future Expansion

Potential future systems:

- advanced emotional simulation;
- family trees;
- NPC politics;
- personal ambitions;
- autonomous settlements.

# Final Statement

The player is not the only person living in this world.

Every NPC has a story.

Every individual has a reason to exist.

The Awakened Soul is not entering an empty world.

They are entering a world that was already alive.
