# Purpose

This document defines the Corruption System within Project Draugr.

It establishes how corruption affects:

- the environment;
- living organisms;
- resources;
- civilizations;
- the overall world state.

The purpose of this system is to create a defining mystery and survival challenge within the world.

---

# Design Philosophy

Corruption is not simply destruction.

It is transformation.

The world was not destroyed.

The world changed into something different.

---

# Core Principle

## Corruption Alters Reality Without Breaking Reality.

Corruption follows rules.

It may be unknown.

It may be dangerous.

It may be misunderstood.

However:

It is not random.

---

# Origin

The true origin of corruption is unknown.

Different civilizations may have different beliefs.

Possible interpretations:

- a natural phenomenon;
- a consequence of an ancient event;
- a failed experiment;
- a cosmic occurrence;
- a punishment;
- an evolution of the world itself.

The truth remains hidden.

# Environmental Corruption

Corruption changes locations.

Examples:

Normal forest:

- stable ecosystem;
- predictable wildlife.

Corrupted forest:

- altered vegetation;
- dangerous organisms;
- unusual environmental behavior.

---

# Biological Corruption

Living organisms may be affected.

Possible effects:

- mutation;
- adaptation;
- behavioral changes;
- physical transformation.

---

# Creature Corruption

Corrupted creatures are not automatically evil.

They are altered beings.

Their behavior depends on:

- species;
- environment;
- corruption influence.

---

# Resource Corruption

Resources may become:

- unusable;
- dangerous;
- altered;
- rare.

Example:

A corrupted plant may become:

- poisonous;
- medicinal;
- valuable material.

---

# Corruption Spread

Corruption may change over time.

Factors affecting spread:

- environment;
- existing corruption;
- unknown mechanisms;
- major world events.

---

# Spread Principles

Corruption should:

- have logical progression;
- be observable;
- create consequences.

It should not randomly appear without cause.

---

# Corruption Levels

Corruption may be classified by severity.

---

## Level 0 — Normal

No visible corruption.

Environment functions normally.

---

## Level 1 — Influence

Minor changes.

Examples:

- unusual growth;
- strange behavior;
- environmental signs.

---

## Level 2 — Alteration

Visible transformation.

Examples:

- changed terrain;
- mutated life;
- unstable resources.

---

## Level 3 — Severe Corruption

Major environmental change.

Examples:

- dangerous ecosystems;
- altered landscapes;
- extreme survival conditions.

---

## Level 4 — Unknown

Areas where normal understanding fails.

Characteristics:

- unpredictable;
- rare;
- connected to deeper mysteries.

---

# Corruption and Survival

The Awakened Soul must learn:

- when to avoid corruption;
- when to study it;
- when to use it.

Corruption may provide:

- rare resources;
- knowledge;
- opportunities.

Danger and value exist together.

## Research

## Worship
