# Purpose

This document defines the regional structure of the world within Project Draugr.

Regions represent large-scale geographic and environmental divisions that naturally exist within the world.

This document defines how regions function.

It does not define individual regions in detail.

---

# Design Philosophy

Regions are not gameplay boundaries.

They are natural divisions created by:

- geography;
- climate;
- history;
- corruption;
- environmental evolution.

The Awakened Soul does not enter a region because a quest sends them there.

They enter because the world physically exists that way.

---

# Core Principle

## The World Is Naturally Divided

The world contains different environments because different conditions create different outcomes.

A region is defined by:

- what exists there;
- how life survives there;
- how dangerous it is;
- how history shaped it.

---

# Regional Structure

The world follows this hierarchy:

```
World

↓

Continents

↓

Regions

↓

Zones

↓

Locations

↓

Points of Interest
```

Regions represent large-scale areas.

---

# Purpose of Regions

Regions provide:

## Environmental Identity

Each region has unique characteristics.

Examples:

- terrain;
- climate;
- resources;
- wildlife;
- corruption level.

---

## Survival Challenges

Different regions create different survival problems.

Examples:

A barren region:

- lack of water;
- limited vegetation;
- extreme exposure.

---

A corrupted region:

- unpredictable mutations;
- dangerous environments;
- altered resources.

---

## Civilization Development

Regions influence where civilizations form.

Survival determines settlement.

Not convenience.

---

# Regional Classification

Regions may be classified by multiple factors.

There is no single category system.

---

# Geographic Classification

Based on physical features:

Examples:

- plains;
- mountains;
- deserts;
- forests;
- oceans;
- islands;
- underground regions.

---

# Environmental Classification

Based on survival conditions:

Examples:

- habitable;
- hostile;
- extreme;
- corrupted;
- unknown.

---

# Historical Classification

Based on past events:

Examples:

- former civilization centers;
- abandoned territories;
- disaster zones;
- ancient lands.

---

# Corruption Influence

Corruption does not create separate regions.

It changes existing regions.

Example:

A forest may become:

Before:

- fertile;
- diverse;
- abundant.

After corruption:

- twisted vegetation;
- dangerous wildlife;
- unstable environment.

The region remains the same place.

It has changed.

---

# The Apocalyptic World

The current world condition is defined by imbalance.

The apocalypse transformed large portions of the planet.

Major environmental characteristics include:

- vast barren lands;
- dangerous oceans;
- damaged ecosystems;
- isolated survival zones;
- corrupted territories.

However:

The world is not completely dead.

Life adapts.

---

# Known and Unknown Regions

Not all regions are equally understood.

A region may be:

## Known

Civilizations have information about it.

---

## Partially Known

Limited exploration exists.

---

## Unknown

Little or no reliable information exists.

---

Unknown does not mean empty.

Unknown means undiscovered.

---

# Region Boundaries

Regions should not have artificial borders.

Transition between regions should feel natural.

Examples:

A forest gradually becomes:

- dry woodland;
- grassland;
- barren terrain.

A corrupted area may slowly spread.

---

# Region Change Over Time

Regions are not permanent.

They can change because of:

- climate;
- corruption;
- civilization activity;
- natural events;
- major world events.

---

# Player Interaction With Regions

The Awakened Soul may:

- explore regions;
- map regions;
- settle regions;
- transform regions;
- avoid regions.

The player does not unlock regions.

They discover them.

# Developer Notes

Regions should support emergent gameplay.

A region should answer:

"Why would something live here?"

"Why would someone settle here?"

"Why would someone avoid this place?"

If those answers do not exist, the region lacks purpose.

---

# Edge Cases

## Empty Regions

A region may contain little life.

However, it should still have:

- environmental identity;
- history;
- potential discovery value.

---

## Player Modification

Large player actions may change regions over time.

Examples:

- establishing settlements;
- destroying ecosystems;
- reducing corruption.

---

## Unknown Regions

Unknown regions must still follow world rules.

Mystery does not mean randomness.
