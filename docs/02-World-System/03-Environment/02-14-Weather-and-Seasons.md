# Purpose

This document defines the Weather and Seasons System within Project Draugr.

It establishes how atmospheric conditions, seasonal changes, and environmental patterns affect the world.

The purpose of this system is to create a living environment where weather influences survival, exploration, and civilization.

---

# Design Philosophy

Weather is not decoration.

Weather is a force.

The Awakened Soul does not control the environment.

They must understand it, adapt to it, and survive within it.

---

# Core Principle

## The World Has Weather Because The World Exists.

Weather occurs independently from player actions.

The Awakened Soul experiences weather.

They do not trigger weather.

---

# Weather System

Weather is determined by:

- location;
- season;
- climate;
- geography;
- corruption influence;
- world events.

---

# Weather Categories

Weather is divided into several categories.

---

# Normal Weather

Natural atmospheric conditions.

Examples:

- clear skies;
- clouds;
- light rain;
- wind;
- fog.

These conditions are common and expected.

---

# Severe Weather

Conditions that create survival challenges.

Examples:

- heavy storms;
- extreme heat;
- blizzards;
- intense rainfall;
- strong winds.

Severe weather requires preparation.

---

# Extreme Weather Events

Rare environmental events.

Examples:

- massive storms;
- prolonged drought;
- extreme temperature shifts;
- destructive weather patterns.

These events may affect entire regions.

---

# Corrupted Weather

Weather influenced by the world's corruption.

Examples:

- unnatural storms;
- contaminated rainfall;
- abnormal atmospheric conditions;
- altered weather cycles.

Corrupted weather should feel different from normal disasters.

---

# Weather Impact

Weather affects multiple systems.

---

# Survival Impact

Weather influences:

- body temperature;
- exhaustion;
- hydration;
- shelter requirements;
- equipment durability.

Example:

Heavy rain may:

- reduce body temperature;
- damage exposed supplies;
- make fire difficult.

---

# Travel Impact

Weather influences:

- visibility;
- movement speed;
- navigation;
- danger levels.

Example:

A sandstorm may:

- hide landmarks;
- reduce navigation accuracy;
- force travelers to seek shelter.

---

# Resource Impact

Weather affects:

- farming;
- water availability;
- plant growth;
- animal behavior.

Example:

A drought may reduce:

- crops;
- natural water sources;
- wildlife population.

---

# Civilization Impact

Settlements adapt to weather.

Examples:

A farming community may:

- store food before winter;
- build irrigation systems;
- migrate during disasters.

---

# Seasonal System

Seasons follow the synchronized world calendar.

The world experiences:

- spring;
- summer;
- autumn;
- winter.

Season behavior depends on region.

---

# Seasonal Variation

Different regions experience seasons differently.

Example:

A tropical region:

- heavy rainfall seasons;
- dry seasons.

A northern region:

- severe winters;
- short summers.

---

# Spring

Common characteristics:

- environmental recovery;
- increased plant growth;
- increased wildlife activity.

Possible effects:

- better farming conditions;
- animal reproduction.

---

# Summer

Common characteristics:

- higher temperatures;
- increased resource activity.

Possible effects:

- drought risk;
- increased water demand.

---

# Autumn

Common characteristics:

- preparation period.

Possible effects:

- harvesting;
- resource collection;
- migration preparation.

---

# Winter

Common characteristics:

- reduced resources;
- harsh conditions.

Possible effects:

- survival difficulty;
- limited travel;
- increased shelter importance.

---

# Corruption Influence On Seasons

Corruption may alter normal seasonal behavior.

Examples:

A corrupted region may experience:

- unnatural cold;
- unstable weather;
- failed agriculture;
- unusual storms.

The world remains logical.

Corruption changes conditions.

It does not remove rules.

---

# Weather Prediction

Knowledge of weather becomes valuable.

The Awakened Soul may learn through:

- observation;
- experience;
- tools;
- NPC knowledge;
- environmental signs.

---

# Weather Preparation

Survival depends on preparation.

Examples:

Before winter:

- store food;
- prepare shelter;
- gather fuel.

Before storms:

- secure structures;
- protect supplies;
- find safe locations.

---

# Weather And Offline Survival

Weather continues while the Awakened Soul is disconnected.

A player logging out must consider:

- current weather;
- upcoming conditions;
- shelter protection.

Example:

Logging out during a calm day does not guarantee safety if a storm arrives later.

---

# Weather As A Historical Event

Major weather events may become part of world history.

Examples:

- legendary storms;
- destroyed settlements;
- changed landscapes.

Future Chronicles may discover evidence of past events.

---

# Developer Notes

Weather should prioritize:

- logical behavior;
- survival impact;
- world immersion.

Avoid weather existing only as visual variation.

Every major weather event should answer:

"What changes because this happened?"

---

# Edge Cases

## Player Shelter

Shelters should provide varying levels of protection.

A wooden shelter is different from a reinforced structure.

---

## Extreme Events

Rare events should be meaningful.

They should not become constant punishment.

---

## Corrupted Areas

Corrupted weather should follow corruption rules.

It should remain mysterious but consistent.

---

# Future Expansion

Possible future systems:

- climate simulation;
- weather forecasting;
- atmospheric research;
- player-built weather protection;
- regional climate change.

---

# Final Statement

The world does not change because the Awakened Soul is watching.

The clouds move.

The storms arrive.

The seasons turn.

The environment continues writing its own story.

The Awakened Soul simply decides how to survive within it.
