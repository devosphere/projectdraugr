# Purpose

This document defines the World Continuity System, the framework responsible for maintaining the existence of the world while an Awakened Soul is not actively connected.

The purpose of this system is to ensure that Project Draugr behaves as a persistent parallel universe rather than a paused game environment.

The world continues.

The Chronicle continues.

Time does not wait.

---

# Design Philosophy

The Awakened Soul is not the center of existence.

They are a participant within a much larger world.

When an Awakened Soul disconnects:

The world does not stop.

The world does not freeze.

The world does not wait.

Events continue according to the natural laws of the universe.

---

# Core Principle

## The World Exists Without Observation

The existence of the world does not depend on whether an Awakened Soul is currently observing it.

Civilizations continue.

Nature continues.

Time continues.

The Awakened Soul simply stops experiencing events directly.

---

# Offline State

When an Awakened Soul disconnects, they remain physically present within the world.

Their last known condition becomes their current state.

The Awakened Soul does not:

- perform actions;
- make decisions;
- gain experience;
- learn skills;
- gather resources;
- interact with others.

They simply exist.

---

# World Simulation During Absence

While an Awakened Soul is offline, the following systems continue.

## Environment

Continues:

- weather changes;
- temperature changes;
- seasons;
- natural events.

---

## Wildlife

Continues:

- movement;
- hunting;
- migration;
- reproduction;
- survival behavior.

---

## Resources

Continues:

- regeneration;
- depletion;
- environmental changes.

---

## Civilization

Continues:

- construction;
- trade;
- movement;
- conflicts;
- population changes.

---

# Awakened Soul Simulation During Absence

The Awakened Soul is affected only by passive conditions.

The system does not simulate active gameplay.

Allowed:

- hunger progression;
- thirst progression;
- fatigue;
- injuries worsening;
- environmental exposure;
- shelter protection.

Not allowed:

- automatic movement;
- combat;
- crafting;
- exploration;
- decision-making.

The Awakened Soul cannot progress while absent.

They can only survive or fail to survive.

---

# Preparation Principle

Before leaving the world, an Awakened Soul must consider their circumstances.

The decision to disconnect becomes part of survival.

Examples:

## Prepared

An Awakened Soul:

- returns to a secure shelter;
- stores food and water;
- avoids environmental danger.

Outcome:

Higher survival probability.

---

## Unprepared

An Awakened Soul:

- sleeps outdoors;
- remains injured;
- lacks supplies;
- stays near danger.

Outcome:

Higher risk of Chronicle termination.

---

# Offline Survival Examples

## Safe Scenario

The Awakened Soul disconnects inside a protected shelter.

Conditions:

- sufficient food;
- sufficient water;
- safe environment.

After several days:

- hunger increases;
- fatigue changes;
- survival continues.

---

## Dangerous Scenario

The Awakened Soul disconnects in an exposed environment.

Conditions:

- severe weather;
- no shelter;
- nearby threats.

Possible outcomes:

- injury;
- worsening condition;
- death.

---

# Death During Absence

If the Awakened Soul dies while offline:

The Chronicle ends.

Upon returning:

The Awakened Soul does not regain consciousness.

Instead, they discover that their Chronicle has concluded.

Possible discoveries:

- remains;
- abandoned equipment;
- journal entries;
- environmental evidence.

The world continues after their death.

---

# Permadeath Philosophy

Permadeath is not intended as punishment.

It represents consequence.

The world respects the same rules whether the Awakened Soul is present or absent.

A life that is neglected can end.

A life that is prepared can continue.

---

# Chronicle Legacy

Future systems may allow previous Chronicles to become part of world history.

Examples:

A future Awakened Soul may discover:

- abandoned shelters;
- graves;
- journals;
- tools;
- buildings;
- reputation;
- stories.

A Chronicle does not disappear.

It becomes history.

---

# World Continuity Rules

The system must follow these principles:

- The world never pauses.
- Time never reverses.
- The Awakened Soul never receives artificial protection.
- Offline actions never occur.
- Consequences remain logical.
- Previous Chronicles leave possible traces.

# Developer Notes

The World Continuity System is one of the defining identity systems of Project Draugr.

Implementation should prioritize logical consistency over punishment.

The goal is not to force constant activity.

The goal is to make every decision meaningful.

Before leaving the world, the Awakened Soul should naturally think:

"Will I still be alive when I return?"

---

# Edge Cases

## Extended Absence

The system must support:

- days;
- weeks;
- months;
- potentially years.

---

## Server Interruptions

Future technical systems must determine how continuity calculations are handled after unavailable periods.

---

## Multiple Awakened Souls

All Awakened Souls exist in the same continuous world.

One person's absence does not pause another person's reality.

---

# Future Expansion

Possible future systems:

- inheritance;
- descendants;
- historical records;
- archaeology;
- Chronicle discoveries;
- legacy reputation.

These systems should transform past lives into world history.

---

# Final Statement

An Awakened Soul may close their eyes.

The world does not.

The rivers continue flowing.

The seasons continue changing.

Civilizations continue growing.

And when the Awakened Soul returns...

The world will have moved forward without them.
