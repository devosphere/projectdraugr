# Purpose

This document defines the calendar and timekeeping system used within Project Draugr.

It establishes how time is measured, recorded, and understood by the world and its inhabitants.

The purpose of this system is to maintain the feeling that Project Draugr is a continuously existing parallel universe.

---

# Design Philosophy

Time is one of the fundamental laws of existence.

The world does not create time for gameplay purposes.

The world experiences time because existence continues.

Every civilization, creature, and Awakened Soul exists within the same continuous timeline.

---

# Core Principle

## One Reality, One Timeline

Project Draugr follows the same universal date and time progression as Earth.

Example:

Earth:

```
July 19, 2026
08:00 PM
```

Project Draugr:

```
July 19, 2026
08:00 PM
```

The worlds share time.

They do not share reality.

---

# Universal Time System

The world uses:

- 24-hour days;
- 60-minute hours;
- 60-second minutes;
- 7-day weeks;
- 12-month years.

The basic measurement of time follows Earth's structure.

---

# Calendar Structure

Project Draugr follows:

## Day

One complete rotation cycle.

Duration:

24 real-world hours.

---

## Week

Seven days.

Days may have different cultural names depending on civilization.

---

## Month

Twelve months per year.

The numerical structure remains consistent with Earth time.

---

## Year

A year represents one complete cycle of seasons.

Duration:

365 days.

Leap years follow the synchronized Earth calendar.

---

# Why Earth Time Is Preserved

The preservation of Earth time is intentional.

The Awakened Soul comes from Earth.

Using familiar time measurement creates an immediate connection between:

- the world they left;
- the world they entered.

The date becomes a reminder that both realities continue simultaneously.

---

# Cultural Interpretation of Time

Although the universal timeline matches Earth, civilizations may interpret time differently.

Different societies may have:

- unique month names;
- historical eras;
- religious calendars;
- traditional holidays;
- cultural events.

The passage of time is universal.

The meaning of time is cultural.

---

# Historical Dating

The world maintains its own historical records.

Civilizations may record events using:

- years since major events;
- dynastic periods;
- disasters;
- discoveries;
- wars.

Example:

A civilization may record:

"Year 243 after the Great Collapse"

while the universal calendar records:

"Year 2026"

Both represent the same moment.

---

# The Current Era

The world exists within a post-apocalyptic era.

The exact naming of this era is civilization-dependent.

Different groups may believe:

- the apocalypse was recent;
- the apocalypse was ancient;
- the apocalypse is still occurring.

The truth is part of future world-building.

---

# Time Perception

The Awakened Soul experiences time normally.

There is no:

- time acceleration;
- time compression;
- artificial waiting;
- separate game clock.

One hour experienced in the world equals one hour in reality.

---

# Aging

All living beings age according to the universal timeline.

This includes:

- Awakened Souls;
- NPCs;
- creatures;
- civilizations.

Time has consequences.

---

# Seasonal Cycle

The calendar controls:

- seasons;
- weather patterns;
- environmental changes;
- agricultural cycles.

Future documentation will define detailed seasonal behavior.

The system must support:

- consistency;
- persistence;
- historical tracking.

Must follow the synchronized Earth calendar.

---

## Player Absence

Calendar time continues normally.

---

## Multiple Awakened Souls

All individuals share the same date and time.

There are no personal timelines.

The world does not count time because the Awakened Soul is watching.

The sun rises.

The seasons change.

Years pass.

The Chronicle simply becomes another moment within the endless timeline.
