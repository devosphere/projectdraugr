# Purpose

This document defines the Real-Time System, the fundamental time synchronization rule governing the relationship between the real world and the world of Project Draugr.

The Real-Time System ensures that the world of Project Draugr exists as a continuous parallel reality rather than a paused game environment.

---

# Design Philosophy

Project Draugr does not exist only when the Awakened Soul is present.

The world continues.

Time passes.

Events occur.

Life changes.

The Awakened Soul is a participant within the world, not the center of it.

The passage of time must feel natural and unavoidable.

---

# Core Principle

The world clock of Project Draugr follows the same date and time as the real world.

Example:

Real World:

```
July 18, 2026
10:00 PM
```

Project Draugr:

```
Same date
Same time
```

The two worlds share synchronized time.

Only the reality is different.

---

# Time Synchronization Rules

The following rules apply:

## Rule I — One Universal Clock

Project Draugr uses a single continuous clock.

There are no:

- separate player instances;
- isolated timelines;
- offline simulations with accelerated time;
- personal time zones.

Every Awakened Soul exists within the same world timeline.

---

## Rule II — Time Never Pauses

The world continues regardless of player activity.

When an Awakened Soul disconnects:

- time continues;
- weather continues;
- creatures continue;
- settlements continue;
- resources change;
- events progress.

The world does not freeze.

---

## Rule III — No Artificial Time Acceleration

The world does not simulate days passing instantly.

One real second equals one second within Project Draugr.

The world follows the same pace as reality.

---

# The Parallel Universe Principle

Project Draugr should feel like another universe existing alongside Earth.

The Awakened Soul may leave for several days in Earth time.

When they return:

The world has changed.

Examples:

A forest fire may have spread.

A settlement may have expanded.

A resource area may have been depleted.

A storm may have damaged structures.

An NPC may have grown older.

The world remembers.

---

# Relationship Between Real Time and Player Experience

Real-time progression creates responsibility.

An Awakened Soul must consider:

- when to travel;
- where to rest;
- how long they will be absent;
- whether their shelter is safe;
- whether resources are sufficient.

Logging out is not equivalent to freezing existence.

---

# Offline State

When an Awakened Soul is not actively connected:

Their physical existence remains within the world.

The world continues around them.

The Awakened Soul does not perform actions while offline.

However:

Their condition may change based on circumstances.

Examples:

Safe:

- sleeping inside a secure shelter;
- protected by companions;
- sufficient food and water available.

Dangerous:

- exposed outdoors;
- injured;
- lacking resources;
- located near hostile creatures.

# Player Absence

Absence is part of the Chronicle.

An Awakened Soul must prepare before leaving the world.

Examples:

Before leaving:

Good preparation:

- secure shelter;
- stored food;
- protected location.

Poor preparation:

- sleeping outdoors;
- low supplies;
- dangerous surroundings.

The outcome is determined by the world.

Not by convenience.

---

# Design Rules

The Real-Time System must never:

- pause because the player disconnects;
- rewind time;
- skip consequences;
- protect the Awakened Soul from their decisions.

The world does not adapt itself to the player's schedule.

The Awakened Soul adapts to the world.
