# Purpose

This document defines the fundamental simulation rules that govern how the world of Project Draugr behaves.

The World Simulation System ensures that events occur through consistent rules rather than scripted outcomes.

The purpose is to create a world that feels alive, unpredictable, and logical.

---

# Design Philosophy

Project Draugr is not a story that happens around the Awakened Soul.

It is a world where stories naturally emerge.

The system does not ask:

"How do we make the player experience a specific event?"

The system asks:

"Given the current state of the world, what would logically happen?"

---

# Core Principle

## The World Reacts, It Does Not Perform

The world does not exist to entertain the Awakened Soul.

Events occur because conditions create possibilities.

The Awakened Soul may influence events.

They do not control reality.

---

# Universal Simulation Laws

The world follows several fundamental laws.

---

# Law I — Cause Creates Effect

Every event must have a logical cause.

Examples:

A forest disappears.

Possible causes:

- corruption spread;
- wildfire;
- resource exploitation;
- environmental change.

A settlement disappears.

Possible causes:

- invasion;
- famine;
- disease;
- migration;
- disaster.

Nothing happens without a reason.

---

# Law II — Actions Create Consequences

Every meaningful action changes the world.

Examples:

The Awakened Soul:

Plants crops.

Result:

- food availability changes;
- local resources change;
- possible settlement growth.

---

The Awakened Soul:

Kills predators in an area.

Result:

- prey population changes;
- ecosystem changes.

---

The Awakened Soul:

Destroys a settlement.

Result:

- survivors react;
- reputation changes;
- history records the event.

---

# Law III — The World Has No Scripted Destiny

The world does not contain a predetermined storyline.

There are:

- no required quests;
- no guaranteed heroes;
- no fixed endings.

Stories emerge from interactions between:

- individuals;
- civilizations;
- environments;
- events.

---

# Law IV — Probability Influences Events

Not everything is guaranteed.

The world operates through probability.

Examples:

A storm may:

- pass safely;
- damage structures;
- destroy weak shelters.

A creature encounter may:

- not happen;
- become hostile;
- become neutral.

Probability creates uncertainty.

---

# Law V — Information Is Limited

Entities within the world only know what they can reasonably know.

NPCs do not possess universal knowledge.

The Awakened Soul does not possess universal knowledge.

The world contains unknown information.

---

# NPC Autonomy

NPCs are independent entities.

They possess:

- needs;
- goals;
- fears;
- relationships;
- memories.

NPCs should not exist only to serve the Awakened Soul.

---

# NPC Decision Principles

NPC behavior considers:

- survival;
- resources;
- relationships;
- environment;
- personal beliefs.

An NPC may:

- help;
- ignore;
- fear;
- distrust;
- oppose;
- cooperate.

---

# Environmental Simulation

The environment responds naturally.

Examples:

## Weather

A storm affects:

- travel;
- visibility;
- agriculture;
- structures.

---

## Terrain

Terrain affects:

- movement;
- resources;
- settlement possibilities.

---

## Resources

Resources are:

- consumed;
- regenerated;
- transformed.

---

# Resource Simulation

Resources follow logical rules.

Examples:

Trees:

Affected by:

- growth rate;
- harvesting;
- environmental conditions.

Animals:

Affected by:

- population;
- food availability;
- predators.

Minerals:

Affected by:

- extraction;
- geological limitations.

---

# Knowledge and Discovery

The world exists regardless of knowledge.

Discovery reveals information.

It does not create information.

Example:

A hidden ruin exists before discovery.

The Awakened Soul only learns of it after investigation.

---

# Emergent Stories

The purpose of simulation is to allow unexpected Chronicles.

Examples:

A farmer:

- discovers a corrupted region;
- studies it;
- develops new methods;
- creates a settlement.

---

A traveler:

- finds abandoned ruins;
- discovers history;
- reconnects lost civilizations.

---

A survivor:

- builds a shelter;
- protects a community;
- becomes part of local history.

---

# Player Freedom

The simulation system must support actions without requiring predefined outcomes.

If an action is:

- physically possible;
- logically consistent;
- achievable within world rules;

the player should be allowed to attempt it.

---

# Impossible Actions

The world should reject actions naturally.

Examples:

Cannot:

- survive without oxygen;
- instantly master impossible skills;
- create resources from nothing.

The world respects its own laws.

# Developer Notes

Simulation should prioritize:

- consistency over convenience;
- logic over scripted outcomes;
- immersion over artificial game rules.

The player should feel:

"The world reacted."

Not:

"The developer activated an event."

- probability;

# Final Statement

The world of Project Draugr does not tell stories.

Every Chronicle is different because every action changes what comes next.

The world does not follow the Awakened Soul.

The world responds.
