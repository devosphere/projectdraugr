# Purpose

This document defines the fundamental structure of the world within Project Draugr.

It establishes how the universe is organized, how environments are separated, and how the world exists as a persistent reality independent from the Awakened Soul.

This document does not define individual locations.

It defines the framework where locations exist.

---

# Design Philosophy

The world of Project Draugr should feel ancient, vast, and indifferent.

The Awakened Soul does not enter a world created for adventure.

They enter a world that has already experienced:

- history;
- collapse;
- survival;
- adaptation;
- loss.

The world was not waiting for them.

They are entering the aftermath of events they do not yet understand.

---

# Core Principle

## The World Is Larger Than Discovery

The known world represents only a fraction of existence.

No Awakened Soul can assume:

- everything has been discovered;
- every region has been explored;
- every civilization is known;
- every mystery has an answer.

The unknown is a permanent part of the world.

---

# Universal World Structure

The world consists of multiple interconnected layers.

```
Universe

↓

World

↓

Continents

↓

Regions

↓

Zones

↓

Locations

↓

Points of Interest
```

Each layer provides increasing detail.

---

# The World

The world is a parallel reality separate from Earth.

It shares:

- the same continuous timeline;
- physical existence;
- natural principles.

It does not share:

- history;
- civilization;
- geography;
- biology;
- events.

---

# Global Environment

The current world state is defined by a post-apocalyptic condition.

The world contains:

- corrupted barren lands;
- dangerous oceans;
- surviving ecosystems;
- isolated civilizations;
- abandoned ruins;
- unknown territories.

The apocalypse did not end the world.

It transformed it.

---

# Land and Ocean

The world contains both:

## Land

Land regions may include:

- surviving natural areas;
- corrupted wastelands;
- abandoned settlements;
- resource zones;
- unexplored territories.

---

## Ocean

The oceans are not empty spaces.

They contain:

- dangers;
- mysteries;
- resources;
- unknown life forms;
- possible remnants of previous civilizations.

The ocean represents one of the largest unexplored parts of the world.

---

# Corruption

Corruption is a major environmental factor.

It affects:

- terrain;
- resources;
- creatures;
- weather;
- civilizations.

Corruption is not simply destruction.

It is a transformation of the world.

Future documentation will define its origin and mechanics.

---

# Civilization Distribution

Civilization does not exist everywhere.

Survival conditions determine settlement locations.

Civilizations may exist in:

- protected areas;
- resource-rich regions;
- ancient structures;
- isolated communities.

Large unified civilizations are rare.

Survival is more common than prosperity.

---

# Exploration Philosophy

The world should encourage exploration through curiosity.

The Awakened Soul should discover:

- places;
- histories;
- resources;
- dangers;
- cultures.

Exploration should never feel like completing a checklist.

---

# World Scale

The world should be large enough that:

- unexplored areas exist;
- civilizations cannot know everything;
- travel has meaning;
- discoveries feel significant.

The exact scale will be determined during technical design.

The principle remains:

The world must feel larger than any single Chronicle.

---

# Player Relationship With The World

The Awakened Soul is:

Not:

- the chosen one;
- the center of history;
- guaranteed importance.

They are:

- a survivor;
- an explorer;
- a potential contributor.

Their importance is created through actions.

---

# Natural Boundaries

The world contains natural limitations.

Examples:

- dangerous oceans;
- corrupted regions;
- extreme environments;
- unknown territories.

These boundaries should create meaningful challenges.

---

# World Persistence

Every location exists whether discovered or not.

Undiscovered locations:

- have history;
- contain resources;
- experience changes;
- follow world rules.

Discovery reveals existing reality.

It does not create it.

# Developer Notes

The world should avoid feeling like a collection of game zones.

Zones exist because the world logically contains them.

The order should be:

World first.

Gameplay second.

# Edge Cases

## Unknown Areas

Unknown does not mean empty.

Unexplored areas must still exist.

---

## Player Discovery

Discovery reveals information.

It does not generate reality.

---

## World Expansion

Future expansions should add to the existing world rather than replace it.
