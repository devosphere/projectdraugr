# Purpose

This document defines the Awakened Soul system within Project Draugr.

It establishes the identity, origin, and role of the player character after being transferred from Earth into the new world.

---

# Design Philosophy

The player does not begin as a hero.

The player begins as a survivor.

They arrive with:

- their memories;
- their body;
- their clothing;
- their experiences.

Everything else must be earned.

---

# Core Principle

## The Awakened Soul Is A Visitor Who Must Become Someone.

The player arrives from another world.

Their future identity is created through actions.

---

# Definition

An Awakened Soul is a human consciousness transferred from Earth into the Project Draugr universe.

The transfer occurs when:

- the player's perception ends on Earth;
- consciousness is separated;
- the player awakens in the new world.

---

# Earth Origin

Every Awakened Soul originates from Earth.

However:

The player does not bring Earth technology into the new world.

---

# Transfer Limitations

The player arrives with only:

- their physical body;
- memories;
- clothing;
- items naturally worn during transfer.

---

# Prohibited Transfer Items

The player does not bring:

- smartphones;
- computers;
- vehicles;
- weapons not being carried;
- modern tools;
- stored inventory.

---

# Reasoning

The purpose is to preserve survival progression.

The player begins with familiarity.

They do not begin with an advantage that breaks the world.

---

# Arrival Process

The arrival follows:

```
Earth Life

↓

Unknown Event

↓

Darkness

↓

Mysterious Voice

↓

Awakening

↓

New World
```

---

# The Mysterious Voice

Before awakening, the player hears a voice.

The player does not know its identity.

The voice may:

- communicate;
- question;
- observe.

The true identity of this voice is the Overseer.

---

# Player Knowledge Restriction

The Awakened Soul does not initially know:

- why they were transferred;
- who transferred them;
- what the Overseer is;
- the purpose of their evaluation.

---

# Initial Assessment

Before arrival, the player undergoes a randomized assessment.

The assessment determines:

- starting circumstances;
- initial equipment;
- possible environmental placement.

---

# Discovery Questions

The player answers a series of randomized questions.

The questions:

- adapt based on previous answers;
- explore personality;
- determine life experience;
- determine practical knowledge.

---

# Assessment Purpose

The questions are not a personality quiz.

They determine survival context.

---

# Example Assessment Categories

## Lifestyle

Determines:

- daily habits;
- physical condition;
- environment familiarity.

---

## Experience

Determines:

- practical knowledge;
- skills;
- previous exposure.

---

## Personality

Determines:

- decision tendencies;
- risk behavior.

---

## Preparation

Determines:

- readiness;
- survival instincts.

---

# Starting Equipment

Starting equipment comes from what the player was wearing during transfer.

---

# Equipment Rules

The player may arrive with:

## Head

Examples:

- hat;
- helmet;
- glasses.

---

## Upper Body

Examples:

- shirt;
- jacket;
- armor-like clothing.

---

## Lower Body

Examples:

- pants;
- shorts;
- protective clothing.

---

## Hands

Examples:

- gloves;
- carried wearable items.

---

## Feet

Examples:

- shoes;
- boots.

---

# Equipment Restrictions

Starting clothing:

- has realistic properties;
- has durability;
- may not be ideal for survival.

---

# Example

A person transferred while wearing:

- office clothing;
- leather shoes;
- wristwatch.

They arrive with:

- clothing;
- watch.

They do not arrive with:

- laptop;
- wallet contents;
- backpack unless worn.

---

# Starting Location

The starting location is randomized.

It is determined by:

- assessment answers;
- world conditions;
- simulation rules.

---

# No Generic Starting Point

Players do not all begin in:

- the same forest;
- the same village;
- the same tutorial area.

---

# Player Identity

The Awakened Soul has:

- name;
- memories;
- appearance;
- history.

Their future identity is created through experience.

---

# No Class System

Project Draugr does not use predefined classes.

The player becomes what they practice.

---

# Skill Development

Skills improve through:

- repeated actions;
- learning;
- mentorship;
- experimentation.

---

# Skill Philosophy

Knowledge must be earned.

The world does not grant abilities because the player selected a class.

---

# Building Skill Example

A player cannot instantly construct advanced structures.

They must:

- learn materials;
- understand construction;
- practice;
- prove capability.

---

# The Overseer's Evaluation

The Overseer observes the Awakened Soul.

Evaluation considers:

- choices;
- adaptation;
- resilience;
- morality;
- growth.

---

# Player Freedom

The Awakened Soul may become:

- explorer;
- builder;
- warrior;
- researcher;
- trader;
- leader;
- wanderer.

No single path is required.

---

# Relationship With The World

The Awakened Soul is affected by:

- civilizations;
- ecosystems;
- corruption;
- time;
- consequences.

---

# Chronicle Identity

Every Awakened Soul creates one Chronicle.

A Chronicle represents:

- one life;
- one journey;
- one history.

---

# Death

When the Awakened Soul dies:

- the Chronicle ends;
- the character cannot return;
- a new Chronicle begins.

---

# Legacy

A previous Awakened Soul may leave:

- buildings;
- journals;
- graves;
- discoveries;
- reputation.

# Final Statement

The Awakened Soul is not a hero sent to save the world.

They are a person given another chance to exist somewhere unknown.

The world does not promise them greatness.

The world only gives them a beginning.

What happens next becomes their Chronicle.
