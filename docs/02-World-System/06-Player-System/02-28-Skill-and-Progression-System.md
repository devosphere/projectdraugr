# Purpose

This document defines the Skill and Progression System within Project Draugr.

It establishes how the Awakened Soul develops abilities through:

- practice;
- experience;
- observation;
- failure;
- teaching;
- real-world application.

The purpose is to create meaningful progression based on earned capability.

---

# Design Philosophy

The player does not level up into competence.

The player becomes competent through living.

---

# Core Principle

## Skills Are Earned Through Proof.

The world does not ask:

"What level are you?"

The world asks:

"What have you actually done?"

---

# No Traditional Class System

Project Draugr does not use predefined classes.

The player is not restricted to:

- warrior;
- mage;
- builder;
- farmer;
- engineer.

---

# Identity Through Action

The player's identity emerges from behavior.

A player becomes:

A builder because they build.

A hunter because they hunt.

A leader because others follow them.

A researcher because they discover.

---

# Skill Categories

Skills are divided into:

```
Knowledge

↓

Practice

↓

Competence

↓

Mastery
```

---

# Knowledge

Knowledge represents understanding.

Sources include:

- Earth memories;
- books;
- observation;
- teaching;
- research.

---

# Knowledge Limitations

Knowledge does not equal ability.

Example:

Knowing how a sword works does not make someone a swordsman.

---

# Practice

Practice develops familiarity.

Examples:

- repeatedly using tools;
- performing tasks;
- experimenting.

---

# Competence

Competence means the player can reliably perform the skill.

Examples:

A competent builder can:

- construct functional structures;
- understand materials;
- avoid common mistakes.

---

# Mastery

Mastery represents exceptional ability.

Requires:

- extensive experience;
- successful application;
- understanding;
- adaptation.

---

# Skill Acquisition

Skills are acquired through:

## Repetition

Performing actions repeatedly.

---

## Observation

Learning from:

- nature;
- others;
- previous mistakes.

---

## Teaching

Learning from:

- NPCs;
- civilizations;
- mentors.

---

## Discovery

Creating new methods.

---

# Skill Growth Factors

Growth depends on:

```
Frequency

+

Difficulty

+

Quality

+

Reflection

+

Feedback
```

---

# Difficulty Matters

Performing easy tasks repeatedly provides limited growth.

Challenging situations create greater improvement.

---

# Failure Learning

Failure is part of progression.

Examples:

A failed construction attempt teaches:

- material limits;
- design problems;
- environmental factors.

---

# Skill Decay

Unused skills may decline.

The player does not permanently retain perfect ability.

---

# Physical Skills

Examples:

- strength;
- endurance;
- coordination;
- combat ability.

Improved through:

- physical activity;
- training;
- survival situations.

---

# Crafting Skills

Examples:

- woodworking;
- metalworking;
- tool creation.

Requires:

- understanding materials;
- practice;
- precision.

---

# Building Skills

Building follows strict progression.

---

# Basic Construction

Requires:

- material understanding;
- simple techniques.

Examples:

- shelter;
- storage;
- basic structures.

---

# Advanced Construction

Requires:

- engineering knowledge;
- experience;
- successful projects.

Examples:

- complex buildings;
- defensive structures;
- infrastructure.

---

# Building Philosophy

The player cannot instantly construct advanced structures because:

"You know what it looks like."

is not the same as:

"You understand how it works."

---

# Survival Skills

Examples:

- fire making;
- hunting;
- navigation;
- resource gathering.

---

# Social Skills

Examples:

- negotiation;
- leadership;
- persuasion;
- diplomacy.

---

# Intellectual Skills

Examples:

- research;
- analysis;
- experimentation.

---

# Skill Specialization

Players naturally specialize through behavior.

Two players may have different strengths.

Example:

Player A:

- excellent builder;
- poor fighter.

Player B:

- excellent hunter;
- poor craftsman.

Neither is superior.

---

# Mentorship System

Advanced knowledge may require teachers.

Sources:

- NPC experts;
- civilizations;
- experienced players;
- discovered records.

---

# Learning From Previous Chronicles

Future systems may allow discovery of:

- journals;
- blueprints;
- research notes.

Previous Chronicles become knowledge sources.

---

# Reputation And Skill Recognition

Civilizations may recognize demonstrated ability.

Examples:

A master builder may receive:

- trust;
- contracts;
- apprentices.

---

# Skill Verification

Certain abilities require proof.

Examples:

A player claiming to be a builder must demonstrate:

- successful structures;
- understanding;
- consistency.

---

# Progression Without Artificial Gates

The world should not say:

"You need level 20."

Instead:

The world should say:

"You are not ready because you lack experience."

# Developer Notes

Avoid creating progression systems that encourage grinding.

A player should improve because they live.

Not because they repeat meaningless actions.

---

# Edge Cases

## Player Knows A Real-World Skill

Knowledge transfers.

Physical mastery does not.

---

## Previous Chronicle Experience

Character memories do not transfer.

Player knowledge remains external.

---

## Exploiting Repetition

The system should evaluate meaningful practice.

# Final Statement

In Project Draugr:

A skill is not a number.

A skill is a story.

Every ability represents:

- time spent;
- mistakes survived;
- lessons learned;
- challenges overcome.

The Awakened Soul does not become powerful because the world grants power.

They become capable because they earned it.
