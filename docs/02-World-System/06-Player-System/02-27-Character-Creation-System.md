# Purpose

This document defines the Character Creation System within Project Draugr.

It establishes how a player's Awakened Soul identity is created before entering the world.

The purpose is to create a personalized survival experience based on the player's previous life rather than traditional RPG selection systems.

---

# Design Philosophy

The player does not create a hero.

They recreate a person.

Their previous life influences:

- starting knowledge;
- physical condition;
- habits;
- instincts;
- possible advantages.

---

# Core Principle

## The Player Does Not Choose Who They Are. They Reveal Who They Were.

The world interprets the player.

The player adapts.

---

# Character Creation Flow

The process follows:

```
Identity Setup

↓

Appearance Creation

↓

Life Assessment

↓

Adaptive Questions

↓

Initial Conditions Calculation

↓

Arrival Preparation

↓

Awakening
```

---

# Identity Setup

The player defines:

- name;
- appearance;
- voice preferences;
- personal identifiers.

---

# Appearance System

Appearance creation determines physical identity.

Includes:

- body structure;
- facial features;
- hairstyle;
- age appearance;
- physical characteristics.

---

# Realistic Body Philosophy

The body should follow realistic limitations.

A player cannot create:

- unrealistic strength;
- impossible proportions;
- biological advantages.

---

# Physical Condition

The player's Earth lifestyle influences starting condition.

Examples:

A physically active person may have:

- better endurance;
- better coordination.

A sedentary person may have:

- lower stamina;
- weaker physical adaptation.

---

# Life Assessment System

Before arrival, the player answers adaptive questions.

The assessment is not a personality test.

It is a life simulation analysis.

---

# Assessment Goals

The system determines:

- practical experience;
- knowledge exposure;
- habits;
- survival tendencies;
- decision patterns.

---

# Question System

The assessment uses approximately:

20 primary questions.

However:

The total number may vary.

Questions adapt based on previous answers.

---

# Adaptive Question Example

Initial question:

"What do you usually do when facing an unfamiliar problem?"

Possible answers:

- research;
- ask others;
- experiment;
- avoid;
- improvise.

Follow-up questions depend on the response.

---

# Assessment Categories

The system evaluates:

```
Knowledge

+

Experience

+

Personality

+

Physical Background

+

Preparation

+

Decision Making
```

---

# Knowledge Assessment

Determines previous exposure to:

- technology;
- science;
- crafts;
- nature;
- problem solving.

---

# Experience Assessment

Determines familiarity with:

- tools;
- environments;
- physical tasks;
- social situations.

---

# Personality Assessment

Determines tendencies.

Examples:

- cautious;
- curious;
- aggressive;
- patient;
- cooperative.

---

# Physical Background Assessment

Determines previous lifestyle.

Examples:

- athlete;
- office worker;
- manual worker;
- outdoor enthusiast.

---

# Preparation Assessment

Determines:

- planning ability;
- risk awareness;
- survival mindset.

---

# Hidden Traits

The system may generate hidden traits.

Examples:

- adaptability;
- observation;
- persistence;
- patience;
- creativity.

---

# Hidden Trait Philosophy

Traits are not permanent bonuses.

They represent tendencies.

Actions can strengthen or weaken them.

---

# Starting Knowledge

The player may have knowledge from Earth.

Examples:

A programmer may understand:

- logic;
- systems;
- problem solving.

A mechanic may understand:

- machines;
- repair concepts.

A farmer may understand:

- crops;
- animals;
- land.

---

# Knowledge Limitations

Knowing something does not mean mastering it.

Example:

A person may know basic construction concepts.

They cannot immediately build advanced structures.

---

# Starting Skill Philosophy

No skill is granted instantly.

Knowledge becomes skill through:

- practice;
- experience;
- mentorship.

---

# Initial Equipment Calculation

The system determines what the player carried during transfer.

Factors:

- assessment;
- selected appearance;
- clothing choices.

---

# Starting Clothing

Possible examples:

- casual clothing;
- work clothing;
- outdoor clothing;
- formal clothing.

---

# Clothing Impact

Clothing affects:

- temperature resistance;
- durability;
- movement;
- protection.

---

# Starting Location Calculation

The arrival point depends on:

- assessment results;
- world conditions;
- simulation rules.

---

# Location Philosophy

The player should not receive a guaranteed safe beginning.

Different beginnings create different Chronicles.

---

- near civilization;

Every background has:

advantages.

and

disadvantages.

The player should discover:

"What kind of person am I in this world?"

The player can change.

A cautious person may become brave.

A weak person may become strong.

A selfish person may become compassionate.

# Developer Notes

Avoid making character creation a min-max system.

The goal is immersion.

---

# Edge Cases

## Player Gives False Answers

The system evaluates behavior after arrival.

Actions reveal truth.

---

## Player Wants A Specific Build

The world does not guarantee one.

---

## Experienced Players

Knowledge from previous Chronicles should not automatically transfer.

---

# Future Expansion

Potential future systems:

- deeper psychological assessment;
- personal memories;
- Earth biography generation;
- family history;
- Chronicle comparison.

---

# Final Statement

Character creation is not the beginning of a game.

It is the final moment of the old life.

The player does not select a destiny.

They arrive with a past.

The new world decides what that past becomes.
