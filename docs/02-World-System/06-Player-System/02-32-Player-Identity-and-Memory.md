# Purpose

This document defines the relationship between:

- the real-world player;
- the Awakened Soul;
- individual Chronicles;
- memories and experiences.

The purpose is to maintain immersion while allowing the player to continue experiencing the world after Chronicle death.

---

# Design Philosophy

A life ends.

A player continues.

The world remembers what happened.

The next life begins without inheriting the previous one's existence.

---

# Core Principle

## The Player Remembers. The Chronicle Does Not.

The person holding the controller remembers previous journeys.

The new Awakened Soul does not.

---

# Identity Layers

Project Draugr contains multiple identity layers.

```
Real Player

↓

Awakened Soul Consciousness

↓

Current Chronicle

↓

World Legacy
```

---

# Real Player Identity

The real player exists outside the universe.

They are:

- the observer;
- the decision maker;
- the one experiencing multiple Chronicles.

---

# Awakened Soul Identity

The Awakened Soul is the consciousness placed into the world.

They have:

- personality;
- memories;
- emotions;
- history.

---

# Chronicle Identity

Each Chronicle is a unique individual existence.

A Chronicle has:

- birth;
- experiences;
- relationships;
- achievements;
- death.

---

# World Legacy Identity

After death, the Chronicle becomes part of the world's history.

Examples:

- monuments;
- records;
- stories;
- discoveries.

---

# Memory Categories

Memory is divided into:

```
Character Memory

+

Player Memory

+

World Memory
```

---

# Character Memory

The memories of the current Awakened Soul.

Examples:

- childhood;
- Earth life;
- personal experiences before arrival.

---

# Player Memory

Knowledge retained by the real player.

Examples:

- previous Chronicle experiences;
- discovered mechanics;
- learned strategies.

---

# World Memory

Information preserved inside the universe.

Examples:

- NPC memories;
- journals;
- historical records.

---

# Previous Chronicle Memory

A new Chronicle does not inherit:

- previous relationships;
- previous skills;
- previous achievements;
- previous personal experiences.

---

# Reason

Every Chronicle must represent an authentic life.

Without separation:

Death loses meaning.

---

# Player Knowledge Problem

The player may know things their character should not.

Example:

The player knows:

"There is a dangerous creature beyond the mountain."

The new character does not.

---

# Immersion Principle

The game does not erase player knowledge.

Instead, it creates situations where:

Knowledge does not equal capability.

---

# Example

The player knows how to build a castle.

The new character:

- lacks materials;
- lacks tools;
- lacks experience;
- lacks workforce.

Knowledge alone is insufficient.

---

# Earth Memory

Every Awakened Soul retains memories from Earth.

These may include:

- language;
- education;
- personal experiences;
- general knowledge.

---

# Earth Knowledge Limitations

Earth knowledge must be adapted.

The new world may have:

- different materials;
- different physics;
- different ecosystems;
- different societies.

---

# Identity Conflict

The Awakened Soul may experience:

- nostalgia;
- confusion;
- attachment;
- loneliness.

---

# Psychological Continuity

The player should feel:

"I am still me."

while the world recognizes:

"This is a new life."

---

# Emotional Attachment

Previous Chronicles create emotional history for the player.

Examples:

A player remembers:

- their first shelter;
- their first companion;
- their first discovery.

---

# Chronicle Separation

The new character does not know:

"Your previous self built this."

They discover it as history.

---

# Discovery Of Previous Lives

A new Chronicle may encounter:

- old homes;
- abandoned tools;
- journals;
- graves.

---

# Player Reaction

The emotional experience comes from:

"I created this."

Not:

"My current character remembers this."

---

# Overseer Perspective

The Overseer understands all Chronicles.

The Overseer observes:

- patterns;
- choices;
- growth.

---

# The Overseer's Evaluation

The Overseer does not judge only individual lives.

It observes the player's journey across Chronicles.

---

# Knowledge Transfer Rules

Knowledge transfer between Chronicles is limited.

Allowed:

- player's external understanding;
- learned strategy.

Not allowed:

- character abilities;
- physical mastery;
- inherited relationships.

---

# Preventing Exploitation

The system prevents:

- instant mastery;
- artificial resurrection;
- consequence avoidance.

---

# Memory And The World Continuity System

The world continues regardless of the player.

Previous actions remain:

- visible;
- discoverable;
- historically relevant.

---

# Integration With Other Systems

## Chronicle System

Defines individual lives.

---

## Reputation System

Preserves social impact.

---

## Skill System

Separates knowledge and ability.

---

## Overseer System

Observes the complete journey.

---

# Developer Notes

The goal is not to punish players for remembering.

The goal is to preserve the meaning of each life.

---

# Edge Cases

## Player Recognizes A Location

The character may not know why it feels familiar.

---

## Player Repeats Previous Strategy

The new Chronicle still requires execution.

---

## Player Creates A Similar Character

The world treats them as separate.

---

# Future Expansion

Potential future systems:

- ancestral memory;
- dreams of previous Chronicles;
- Chronicle archives;
- reincarnation theories;
- Overseer revelations.

---

# Final Statement

The player is a traveler across lives.

Each Chronicle is temporary.

Each experience is permanent.

The player carries memories.

The world carries history.

Together, they create the complete story of Project Draugr.
