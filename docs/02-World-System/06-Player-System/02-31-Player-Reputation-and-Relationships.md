# Purpose

This document defines how the world perceives and remembers the Awakened Soul.

It establishes systems for:

- reputation;
- trust;
- relationships;
- social consequences;
- historical recognition.

---

# Design Philosophy

The Awakened Soul is not defined only by what they achieve.

They are defined by how they affect others.

---

# Core Principle

## People Remember Actions More Than Titles.

The world does not care what the player claims to be.

The world remembers what the player actually did.

---

# Reputation Definition

Reputation represents the collective perception of the Awakened Soul.

It is influenced by:

- actions;
- decisions;
- relationships;
- achievements;
- failures.

---

# Reputation Is Not A Number

Reputation should not be a single global value.

Different groups may have different opinions.

---

# Example

A player may be:

Respected by:

- builders;
- farmers;
- explorers.

Disliked by:

- merchants;
- a rival settlement;
- a damaged civilization.

---

# Reputation Layers

Reputation exists across multiple levels.

```
Individual

↓

Community

↓

Settlement

↓

Civilization

↓

World History
```

---

# Individual Reputation

Personal relationships.

Examples:

- friendship;
- trust;
- fear;
- resentment.

---

# Community Reputation

How a local group perceives the player.

Examples:

A village may see the player as:

- protector;
- outsider;
- threat.

---

# Settlement Reputation

Larger social recognition.

Examples:

The player's actions may influence:

- trade access;
- leadership opportunities;
- cooperation.

---

# Civilization Reputation

Long-term cultural perception.

Examples:

The player may become:

- respected figure;
- historical character;
- enemy.

---

# Relationship System

Relationships represent connections between individuals.

---

# Relationship Types

Examples:

## Friendship

Created through:

- support;
- trust;
- shared experiences.

---

## Respect

Created through:

- demonstrated ability;
- reliability;
- achievements.

---

## Fear

Created through:

- violence;
- intimidation;
- destructive actions.

---

## Hatred

Created through:

- betrayal;
- harm;
- personal loss.

---

## Curiosity

Created through:

- unusual behavior;
- unknown origins;
- discoveries.

---

# Relationship Development

Relationships evolve over time.

Factors include:

- interactions;
- promises;
- actions;
- consistency.

---

# Memory System

NPCs remember significant interactions.

Memory may include:

- helpful actions;
- harmful actions;
- promises;
- important events.

---

# Memory Importance

Not every interaction creates permanent memory.

Important events have greater impact.

---

# Example

Giving someone food once:

May create gratitude.

Saving their family:

May create lifelong loyalty.

---

# Trust System

Trust determines willingness to cooperate.

Trust affects:

- conversations;
- trade;
- assistance;
- information sharing.

---

# Building Trust

Trust is created through:

- reliability;
- honesty;
- helping others;
- keeping promises.

---

# Losing Trust

Trust decreases through:

- betrayal;
- dishonesty;
- abandonment;
- violence.

---

# Social Consequences

Actions create reactions.

Examples:

Helping settlements:

May create:

- allies;
- support;
- reputation.

---

Destroying settlements:

May create:

- enemies;
- fear;
- historical hatred.

---

# Reputation Transfer

Stories spread.

Information may travel through:

- merchants;
- travelers;
- civilizations;
- written records.

---

# Reputation Accuracy

Stories may change.

Different groups may remember events differently.

---

# Example

A player who destroyed a corrupted area may be remembered as:

A hero by survivors.

A destroyer by those who lost resources.

---

# Relationship With Previous Chronicles

Previous Chronicles may influence future perception.

Future systems may allow:

- family records;
- historical reputation;
- discovered journals.

---

# Player Legacy

A living player can already create legacy.

Examples:

- founding settlements;
- teaching skills;
- creating discoveries;
- influencing culture.

---

# Reputation And Civilization Politics

High reputation may create opportunities.

Examples:

- leadership roles;
- diplomatic influence;
- cooperation.

---

# Low Reputation Effects

Negative reputation may result in:

- distrust;
- restricted access;
- hostility.

---

# Reputation Decay

Time affects memory.

However:

Major events may survive generations.

---

# Chronicle Impact

A Chronicle's reputation may continue after death.

Examples:

"The builder who created the mountain fortress."

"The wanderer who disappeared beyond the corrupted lands."

---

---

## Culture System

---

# Developer Notes

Avoid making reputation purely positive or negative.

Complex characters create better stories.

A player may be loved by some and hated by others.

---

# Edge Cases

## Player Performs Hidden Actions

Unknown actions should not create public reputation.

---

## False Rumors

Civilizations may spread incorrect information.

---

## Forgotten Actions

Minor actions may disappear over time.

---

# Future Expansion

Potential future systems:

- diplomacy;

- family relationships;
- faction reputation;
- historical reputation database.

---

# Final Statement

The Awakened Soul leaves footprints everywhere.

Some are physical.

Some are emotional.

Some become stories.

The world may forget a person's face.

But it rarely forgets how that person made them feel.
