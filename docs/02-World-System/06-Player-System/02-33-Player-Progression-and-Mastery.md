# Purpose

This document defines the long-term progression model of the Awakened Soul.

It establishes how a player develops from:

- inexperienced survivor;
- capable individual;
- skilled practitioner;
- recognized expert;
- historical figure.

---

# Design Philosophy

Progression is not becoming stronger because time passed.

Progression is becoming more capable because experience changed the person.

---

# Core Principle

## Time Creates Opportunity. Action Creates Growth.

Simply existing does not create mastery.

Meaningful experience does.

---

# Traditional RPG Rejection

Project Draugr does not primarily use:

- experience points;
- character levels;
- instant skill unlocks;
- class advancement.

---

# Progression Model

Progression follows:

```
Exposure

↓

Learning

↓

Practice

↓

Competence

↓

Mastery

↓

Recognition

↓

Legacy
```

---

# Exposure

The player encounters something new.

Examples:

- new materials;
- new creatures;
- new civilizations;
- new techniques.

---

# Learning

The player gains understanding.

Sources:

- observation;
- experimentation;
- teaching;
- documentation.

---

# Practice

The player repeatedly applies knowledge.

Practice develops:

- efficiency;
- accuracy;
- confidence.

---

# Competence

The player becomes reliable.

A competent person can perform tasks consistently.

---

# Mastery

Mastery represents exceptional understanding.

A master can:

- adapt;
- innovate;
- teach others.

---

# Mastery Requirements

Mastery requires:

```
Time

+

Experience

+

Difficulty

+

Understanding

+

Successful Application
```

---

# No Instant Mastery

The player cannot become an expert through:

- reading one book;
- watching one demonstration;
- receiving one lesson.

---

# Example: Construction

A new player may understand:

"Buildings require foundations."

A skilled builder understands:

- materials;
- structure;
- environment.

A master builder understands:

- efficiency;
- innovation;
- long-term durability.

---

# Example: Combat

A new player knows:

"Attack and defend."

A skilled fighter understands:

- timing;
- positioning;
- opponents.

A master understands:

- strategy;
- adaptation;
- prediction.

---

# Expertise Recognition

Mastery becomes visible through achievement.

Recognition may come from:

- NPCs;
- civilizations;
- apprentices;
- historical records.

---

# Titles

Titles are earned naturally.

Examples:

- Master Builder;
- Great Explorer;
- Renowned Hunter;
- Scholar of Ancient Knowledge.

---

# Titles Are Not Classes

Titles represent reputation.

They do not restrict future actions.

---

# Becoming Legendary

A person becomes legendary through impact.

Examples:

A builder becomes legendary by:

- creating impossible structures;
- influencing architecture;
- teaching generations.

---

An explorer becomes legendary by:

- discovering unknown regions;
- mapping the world;
- surviving impossible journeys.

---

# Individual Progression Paths

Different Chronicles develop differently.

Examples:

## Survivor Path

Focus:

- endurance;
- adaptation;
- self-reliance.

---

## Builder Path

Focus:

- construction;
- engineering;
- settlement development.

---

## Explorer Path

Focus:

- discovery;
- navigation;
- unknown territories.

---

## Scholar Path

Focus:

- research;
- understanding;
- documentation.

---

## Leader Path

Focus:

- diplomacy;
- organization;
- civilization influence.

---

# No Optimal Path

Every path contributes to the world.

---

# Mastery Decay

Skills may weaken without use.

However:

Deep understanding remains.

---

# Example:

A retired blacksmith may lose physical strength.

But still understand:

- metal properties;
- techniques;
- craftsmanship.

---

# Teaching And Legacy

A master can transfer knowledge.

Methods:

- apprenticeships;
- books;
- demonstrations;
- institutions.

---

# Civilization Impact

A player's mastery can influence society.

Examples:

A farmer discovers improved agriculture.

A builder introduces new construction methods.

A researcher discovers unknown materials.

---

# Chronicle Recognition

The world may remember exceptional Chronicles.

Records may include:

- name;
- achievements;
- discoveries;
- lifespan.

---

# Mastery And Death

Death does not erase mastery.

The knowledge may remain through:

- students;
- journals;
- structures;
- cultural practices.

---

# Mastery Across Chronicles

Previous player knowledge does not grant automatic mastery.

However:

The player may understand strategies better.

The new Chronicle must still earn capability.

---

# Relationship With The Overseer

The Overseer observes growth.

Not only outcomes.

The Overseer values:

- adaptation;
- perseverance;
- learning.

---

# Integration With Other Systems

## Skill System

Defines ability development.

---

## Reputation System

Defines recognition.

---

## Civilization System

Defines societal impact.

---

## Chronicle System

Preserves achievements.

---

# Developer Notes

Avoid making progression feel like grinding.

A player should look back and think:

"I became this person."

Not:

"I filled a progress bar."

---

# Edge Cases

## Player Repeats The Same Action

Meaningless repetition should provide minimal improvement.

---

## Player Learns From Books

Knowledge improves.

Mastery still requires practice.

---

## Player Has Previous Chronicle Experience

Player understanding improves.

Character ability does not transfer.

---

# Future Expansion

Potential future systems:

- mastery certification;
- legendary artifacts;
- civilization recognition;
- historical archives;
- apprentice systems.

---

# Final Statement

In Project Draugr:

A level is temporary.

A number is invisible.

A legacy is permanent.

The greatest progression is not becoming stronger.

It is becoming someone the world remembers.
