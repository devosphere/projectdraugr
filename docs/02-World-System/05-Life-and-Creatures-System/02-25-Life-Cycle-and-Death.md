# Purpose

This document defines the Life Cycle and Death System within Project Draugr.

It establishes how all living beings experience:

- birth;
- growth;
- aging;
- survival;
- decline;
- death.

The purpose of this system is to ensure that life and death are meaningful parts of the world simulation.

---

# Design Philosophy

Life is valuable because it can be lost.

The world should not treat living beings as permanent objects.

Every existence has:

- a beginning;
- a journey;
- an ending.

---

# Core Principle

## Death Does Not Remove Existence. It Changes Its Form.

A living being may end.

Its impact may continue.

---

# Universal Life Cycle

All life follows:

```
Creation / Birth

↓

Growth

↓

Maturity

↓

Experience

↓

Decline

↓

Death

↓

Legacy
```

---

# Birth

New life may appear through natural processes.

Examples:

- creature reproduction;
- NPC births;
- civilization generations.

Birth depends on:

- environment;
- resources;
- survival conditions.

---

# Growth

Young beings develop over time.

Growth may affect:

- abilities;
- knowledge;
- behavior;
- social role.

---

# Maturity

Mature beings contribute to their environment.

Examples:

Animals:

- hunting;
- reproduction;
- territory.

Intelligent beings:

- professions;
- leadership;
- knowledge transfer.

---

# Aging

Living beings experience aging.

Effects may include:

- physical decline;
- reduced capability;
- accumulated knowledge.

Age affects each species differently.

---

# Natural Death

Death may occur through:

- old age;
- illness;
- injury;
- environmental conditions.

Natural death is part of world continuity.

---

# Player Death

The Awakened Soul follows the same universal law.

The player is not exempt from mortality.

Death may occur through:

- starvation;
- dehydration;
- combat;
- environment;
- corruption;
- prolonged offline absence.

---

# Permadeath Principle

Project Draugr uses a true Chronicle system.

When an Awakened Soul dies:

- the Chronicle ends;
- the character cannot continue;
- a new Chronicle must begin.

---

# Purpose Of Permadeath

Permadeath exists to create:

- meaningful decisions;
- preparation;
- attachment;
- consequences.

A life without risk has no weight.

---

# Death And The World

Death changes the world.

Examples:

A deceased survivor may leave:

- a grave;
- a structure;
- journals;
- tools;
- reputation;
- discovered knowledge.

---

# Chronicle Legacy

A dead Chronicle does not disappear.

It becomes history.

Future Awakened Souls may discover:

- previous settlements;
- abandoned equipment;
- written records;
- monuments;
- stories.

---

# NPC Death

NPCs may also die.

Death may affect:

- families;
- communities;
- leadership;
- civilization memory.

---

# Civilization Reaction To Death

Civilizations respond differently.

Possible reactions:

- mourning;
- remembrance;
- revenge;
- adaptation.

---

# Creature Death

Creature death affects ecosystems.

Examples:

Removing predators:

May increase prey populations.

Removing prey:

May affect predator survival.

---

# Extinction

Death may happen at larger scales.

Species or civilizations may disappear.

However:

Their existence leaves traces.

---

# Remains And Evidence

Death creates physical evidence.

Examples:

- bones;
- graves;
- ruins;
- abandoned structures;
- records.

These become part of exploration.

---

# Memory System

The world remembers significant lives.

Memory may exist through:

- NPC stories;
- civilization records;
- monuments;
- locations.

---

# Death And The Overseer

The Overseer observes life cycles.

Death may be part of evaluation.

The Overseer does not judge only survival.

It observes:

- choices;
- growth;
- actions;
- consequences.

---

# Offline Death

Because the world continues:

A player may die while disconnected.

Examples:

Logging out:

- without shelter;
- without food;
- inside danger zones;

may result in death before returning.

---

# Death Prevention Philosophy

The game should not prevent death artificially.

Instead, it should provide:

- knowledge;
- preparation;
- warning signs.

Survival is earned.

---

# Death Notification Philosophy

Death should feel meaningful.

The player should understand:

- what happened;
- why it happened;
- what remains.

---

# Legacy Between Chronicles

Future characters may encounter previous lives.

Examples:

A new Awakened Soul discovers:

- an old farmhouse;
- a journal;
- a grave;
- a forgotten achievement.

The previous player is gone.

But the world remembers.

---

Records individual lives.

# Developer Notes

Avoid making death feel like punishment.

Death should create emotional weight.

The player should think:

"I should have prepared better."

Not:

"The game cheated me."

---

# Edge Cases

## Accidental Death

The cause should always be explainable.

---

## Player Absence

Offline survival rules apply.

---

## Civilization Loss

Destroyed societies should leave historical traces.

---

# Future Expansion

Potential future systems:

- inheritance systems;
- ancestral reputation;
- family lines;
- historical records;
- legacy discoveries.

---

# Final Statement

Every life in Project Draugr has one Chronicle.

Some Chronicles are long.

Some are short.

Some are forgotten.

Some become legends.

But none are meaningless.

Death is not the end of a story.

It is the moment when a story becomes history.
