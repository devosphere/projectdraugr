# Purpose

This document defines the Culture and Belief System within Project Draugr.

It establishes how civilizations develop:

- traditions;
- values;
- interpretations;
- social identities;
- beliefs about the world.

The purpose of this system is to create civilizations that feel unique and historically developed.

---

# Design Philosophy

Civilizations do not only survive through resources.

They survive through meaning.

When people experience an unknown world, they create explanations.

Those explanations become:

- traditions;
- religions;
- philosophies;
- cultures.

---

# Core Principle

## Truth Is Universal. Understanding Is Personal.

The universe has objective reality.

However, every civilization interprets reality through:

- experience;
- history;
- environment;
- limited knowledge.

---

# Culture Definition

Culture represents the shared identity of a civilization.

It includes:

- customs;
- traditions;
- language;
- values;
- behaviors;
- social expectations.

---

# Belief Definition

Beliefs are explanations or interpretations about existence.

Beliefs may concern:

- creation;
- corruption;
- death;
- the Overseer;
- the future;
- morality.

---

# Cultural Formation

Culture develops through:

## Environment

A civilization's surroundings influence behavior.

Example:

An ocean civilization may value:

- navigation;
- exploration;
- marine knowledge.

---

## History

Past events shape identity.

Example:

A civilization that survived a catastrophe may value:

- preparation;
- preservation;
- caution.

---

## Threats

Danger creates adaptation.

Example:

A civilization near corrupted land may develop:

- rituals;
- warnings;
- protective traditions.

---

# Cultural Evolution

Cultures change over time.

Changes may occur through:

- discoveries;
- conflicts;
- migrations;
- disasters;
- influential individuals.

Culture is not static.

---

# Belief Systems

Beliefs may develop around major unknowns.

Examples:

- origin of the world;
- purpose of existence;
- corruption;
- Overseer encounters.

---

# The Overseer Interpretation

The Overseer exists.

However, civilizations may understand it differently.

Possible interpretations:

## Creator Belief

The Overseer created existence.

---

## Guardian Belief

The Overseer protects and observes life.

---

## Trial Belief

The Overseer tests living beings.

---

## Fear-Based Belief

The Overseer is seen as a dangerous force.

---

## Mythological Belief

The Overseer is considered an ancient legend.

---

# Corruption Interpretation

Civilizations may have different explanations.

Examples:

## Curse Theory

Corruption is punishment for past actions.

---

## Evolution Theory

Corruption is the next stage of life.

---

## Disaster Theory

Corruption is a natural event.

---

## Divine Theory

Corruption is connected to higher powers.
