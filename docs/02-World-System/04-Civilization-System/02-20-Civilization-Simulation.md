# Purpose

This document defines how civilizations evolve and operate over time within Project Draugr.

It establishes the rules that allow societies to continue existing independently from the Awakened Soul.

The purpose of this system is to create civilizations that feel like living entities within a persistent universe.

---

# Design Philosophy

Civilizations are not static objects.

They are ongoing processes.

A settlement should not freeze when the Awakened Soul leaves.

A civilization should continue experiencing:

- growth;
- struggles;
- adaptation;
- decline.

---

# Core Principle

## History Does Not Pause When Nobody Is Watching.

Civilizations continue existing because they are composed of living beings.

The absence of the Awakened Soul does not stop the world.

---

# Civilization Simulation Model

Civilization behavior is influenced by:

```
Population

+

Resources

+

Environment

+

Leadership

+

Culture

+

Threats

+

Knowledge
```

The interaction of these factors determines civilization outcomes.

---

# Population Simulation

Population is a fundamental civilization factor.

Population changes through:

## Growth

Caused by:

- successful survival;
- available resources;
- stable environment.

---

## Decline

Caused by:

- famine;
- disease;
- conflict;
- disasters;
- corruption.

---

## Migration

Population may move because of:

- resource shortage;
- environmental danger;
- opportunities elsewhere.

---

# Generational Change

Civilizations experience generations.

Over time:

- leaders are replaced;
- knowledge changes;
- traditions evolve;
- beliefs transform.

A civilization should not remain identical forever.

---

# Leadership Simulation

Leadership affects civilization direction.

Leaders may have:

- personalities;
- goals;
- values;
- relationships.

Leadership changes through:

- death;
- succession;
- conflict;
- social events.

---

# Leadership Influence

Different leaders may create different outcomes.

Examples:

A defensive leader:

May focus on:

- walls;
- security;
- isolation.

---

An expansionist leader:

May focus on:

- exploration;
- territory;
- resource acquisition.

---

A knowledge-focused leader:

May focus on:

- research;
- discovery;
- preservation.

---

# Resource Simulation

Civilizations depend on resources.

Important resources include:

- food;
- water;
- materials;
- knowledge;
- trade goods.

---

# Resource Management

Civilizations must decide:

- production;
- consumption;
- storage;
- trade.

Poor management may cause decline.

---

# Economic Simulation

Civilizations may develop economic systems.

Possible activities:

- farming;
- crafting;
- mining;
- trading;
- research.

Economies are shaped by:

- environment;
- culture;
- available technology.

---

# Trade Simulation

Civilizations may establish trade relationships.

Trade depends on:

- distance;
- safety;
- resources;
- trust.

Trade routes may:

- expand;
- disappear;
- become dangerous.

---

# Conflict Simulation

Conflict emerges from conditions.

Possible causes:

- resource scarcity;
- territorial disputes;
- cultural differences;
- historical conflicts.

Conflict should have logical reasons.

---

# War Simulation

Large conflicts may affect:

- populations;
- settlements;
- resources;
- history.

War is not random content.

It is a consequence.

---

# Disaster Simulation

Civilizations may experience:

- storms;
- corruption events;
- environmental changes;
- resource failures.

Their response determines survival.

---

# Knowledge Simulation

Knowledge changes over time.

Civilizations may:

- discover;
- preserve;
- lose;
- misunderstand information.

---

# Technology Development

Technology progresses based on:

- available resources;
- knowledge;
- environmental needs.

Progress is not guaranteed.

A civilization may lose capabilities.

---

# Cultural Evolution

Civilizations change beliefs over generations.

Changes may occur because of:

- discoveries;
- disasters;
- influential individuals;
- external contact.

---

# Settlement Expansion

Successful settlements may expand.

Expansion requires:

- population;
- resources;
- security;
- leadership.

---

# Settlement Collapse

Settlements may decline.

Possible causes:

- abandonment;
- destruction;
- migration;
- resource exhaustion.

Collapsed settlements become part of history.

---

# Civilization Memory

Civilizations remember significant events.

Memory may include:

- heroes;
- disasters;
- wars;
- discoveries;
- previous Chronicles.

---

# Interaction With Awakened Souls

The Awakened Soul can influence civilization simulation.

Examples:

Helping a settlement:

May create:

- friendship;
- reputation;
- historical recognition.

---

Damaging a settlement:

May create:

- fear;
- hatred;
- future conflicts.

---

# Offline Civilization Simulation

Civilizations continue while the Awakened Soul is offline.

When the player returns:

Possible changes:

- new leaders;
- changed relationships;
- expanded settlements;
- new conflicts;
- forgotten promises.

- stories.
