# Purpose

This document defines the Settlement System within Project Draugr.

It establishes how communities are created, maintained, developed, and affected by the world.

The purpose of this system is to create settlements that feel like living places rather than static gameplay locations.

---

# Design Philosophy

Settlements represent survival made physical.

Every wall built, every shelter created, and every community formed represents a decision:

"Life continues here."

A settlement is not defined by size.

It is defined by purpose.

---

# Core Principle

## A Settlement Is A Living Entity.

A settlement has:

- needs;
- resources;
- history;
- population;
- risks;
- potential.

It can succeed.

It can fail.

---

# Settlement Definition

A settlement is a location where intelligent beings establish a semi-permanent presence.

Examples:

- temporary camps;
- villages;
- towns;
- cities;
- fortresses;
- research facilities.

---

# Settlement Types

Settlements vary based on purpose.

---

# Survival Settlement

Created primarily for safety.

Characteristics:

- basic shelter;
- food storage;
- limited population;
- defensive focus.

---

# Farming Settlement

Focused on food production.

Characteristics:

- agriculture;
- livestock;
- water management;
- resource planning.

---

# Trading Settlement

Focused on exchange.

Characteristics:

- merchants;
- storage;
- travel routes;
- economic activity.

---

# Research Settlement

Focused on knowledge.

Characteristics:

- laboratories;
- archives;
- experimentation.

---

# Defensive Settlement

Focused on protection.

Characteristics:

- walls;
- guards;
- strategic location.

---

# Player-Created Settlement

Created by an Awakened Soul.

May begin as:

- shelter;
- camp;
- workshop.

May eventually become:

- village;
- community;
- civilization.

---

# Settlement Requirements

Settlements require:

## Location

A suitable place to exist.

Factors:

- resources;
- safety;
- environment.

---

## Resources

Required resources include:

- food;
- water;
- materials;
- energy or equivalent systems.

---

## Population

Settlements require people.

Population affects:

- productivity;
- defense;
- growth.

---

## Stability

A settlement needs:

- organization;
- leadership;
- cooperation.

# Settlement Development

Settlements may evolve.

Example:

```
Temporary Shelter

↓

Camp

↓

Outpost

↓

Village

↓

Town

↓

City
```

Growth depends on:

- resources;
- population;
- safety;
- knowledge.

---

# Settlement Decline

Settlements may deteriorate.

Causes:

- resource shortage;
- corruption;
- attacks;
- disasters;
- population loss.

---

# Settlement Destruction

A settlement can be destroyed.

However:

Destruction does not erase history.

The remains may become:

- ruins;
- exploration locations;
- historical evidence.

---

# Settlement Simulation

Settlements continue existing without player presence.

They experience:

- resource consumption;
- population changes;
- internal events;
- external threats.

---

# Offline World Interaction

The Awakened Soul's settlement continues while offline.

Examples:

A player builds a farm.

After seven days away:

Possible outcomes:

- successful harvest;
- crop failure;
- resource shortage;
- attack;
- settlement growth.

The world does not pause.

## Trade

---

---

## Competition

## Conflict

# Settlement Reputation

- assistance;
- betrayal;

# Settlement Knowledge

Settlements preserve information.

Examples:

- books;
- journals;
- records;
- stories;

# Chronicle Legacy

A player's settlement may survive beyond their Chronicle.

Examples:

- builds a home;
- creates a village;
- dies.

- inhabit it;
- discover it;
- preserve it;
- forget it.

The player's life becomes part of world history.
