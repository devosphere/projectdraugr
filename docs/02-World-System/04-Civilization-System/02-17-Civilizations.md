# Purpose

This document defines the Civilization System within Project Draugr.

It establishes how intelligent societies exist, develop, adapt, and interact within the world.

The purpose of this system is to create civilizations that exist independently from the Awakened Soul.

---

# Design Philosophy

Civilizations are not quest providers.

They are living societies.

They have:

- histories;
- struggles;
- traditions;
- conflicts;
- ambitions;
- fears.

The Awakened Soul enters their world.

Their world does not revolve around the Awakened Soul.

---

# Core Principle

## Civilizations Exist Before The Chronicle Begins.

Every civilization has:

- a past;
- a present;
- a possible future.

The Awakened Soul may influence their future.

They do not create their existence.

---

# Definition of Civilization

A civilization is a group of intelligent beings organized around:

- survival;
- cooperation;
- shared identity;
- social structure.

A civilization does not require:

- large cities;
- advanced technology;
- written language.

A small isolated community can be a civilization.

---

# Civilization Formation

Civilizations form because conditions allow survival.

Factors include:

- resources;
- environment;
- protection;
- population;
- leadership;
- shared purpose.

---

# Civilization Development

Civilizations may evolve through stages.

Example:

```
Survival Group

↓

Settlement

↓

Community

↓

Established Civilization

↓

Regional Power
```

Progress is not guaranteed.

A civilization may:

- grow;
- stagnate;
- decline;
- disappear.

---

# Civilization Identity

Every civilization should have unique characteristics.

Identity is influenced by:

## Environment

A desert civilization develops differently from an ocean civilization.

---

## History

Past events shape beliefs and behavior.

---

## Resources

Available resources influence development.

---

## Threats

Survival challenges create adaptation.

Focus:

- food;

- protection.

---

## Expansion

- territory;

- influence.

---

## Knowledge

- research;
- discovery;

## Restoration

Focus:

- protecting remaining life and history.

# Civilization Relationships

Civilizations interact with each other.

Possible relationships:

## Cooperation

Shared resources and protection.

---

## Trade

Exchange of:

- materials;
- knowledge;
- services.

---

## Competition

Conflict over:

- territory;
- resources;
- beliefs.

---

## Hostility

Open conflict or survival threats.

---

# Civilization Conflict

Conflict should have logical causes.

Examples:

- resource shortage;
- ideological differences;
- historical disputes;
- environmental pressure.

Conflict should not exist only to create enemies.

---

# Civilization Survival

Civilizations must manage:

- food;
- population;
- security;
- environment;
- resources.

A civilization that ignores reality may decline.

---

# Civilization Memory

Civilizations remember.

They may preserve:

- historical events;
- heroes;
- disasters;
- enemies;
- discoveries.

The actions of previous Chronicles may eventually become part of civilization history.

---

# Interaction With The Awakened Soul

Civilizations may view the Awakened Soul differently.

Possible interpretations:

- stranger;
- survivor;
- opportunity;
- threat;
- myth;
- important individual.

The reaction depends on:

- actions;
- reputation;
- knowledge;
- cultural beliefs.

---

# Civilization Evolution

Civilizations change over time.

Changes may occur because of:

- leadership changes;
- disasters;
- wars;
- discoveries;
- player influence.

---

# Civilization Extinction

Civilizations may disappear.

Causes:

- starvation;
- corruption;
- conflict;
- environmental collapse;
- migration.

However:

Their existence leaves traces.

Examples:

- ruins;
- records;
- traditions;
- descendants.

---

# Civilization and the Overseer

The Overseer observes all life.

Civilizations may unknowingly interact with the influence of the Overseer through:

- myths;
- legends;
- unexplained events.

Different civilizations may interpret the same phenomenon differently.

# Developer Notes

Civilizations should never feel like static factions.

They should:

- make decisions;
- experience problems;
- change over time.

The player should be able to return after months and find:

- leaders changed;
- settlements expanded;
- conflicts started;
- civilizations evolved.

# Final Statement

Civilizations are proof that life continues.

The world of Project Draugr is not only a place of survival.

It is a place where beings struggle to build meaning after the end of their old world.

The Awakened Soul is not entering an empty apocalypse.

They are entering a world that refused to die.
