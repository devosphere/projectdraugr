# Purpose

The Game Design Document (GDD) defines the complete framework that governs Project Draugr.

Unlike traditional game design documents that focus primarily on mechanics, features, and player interactions, Project Draugr's GDD defines the structure of a living simulated reality.

This document establishes:

- the laws governing existence;
- the structure of the world;
- the evolution of civilizations;
- the behavior of life;
- the progression of knowledge;
- the relationship between player and world;
- the principles that guide simulation.

Project Draugr is not designed as a collection of gameplay systems.

It is designed as a framework where gameplay naturally emerges from the interaction between:

- reality;
- life;
- consciousness;
- civilization;
- history;
- evolution;
- choice.

The player's experience is a consequence of entering this world.

The world does not exist for the player.

The player exists within the world.

# Simulation Principles

Project Draugr follows several fundamental simulation principles.

---

# Principle 1 — Simulation Is Authoritative

The simulation determines reality.

Narrative does not override simulation.

Events happen because systems produce them.

---

# Principle 2 — Emergence Over Scripting

The world should create unexpected outcomes.

Instead of:

Developer writes:
"The kingdom falls."

The simulation creates:

Economic collapse

Political instability

Resource shortage

Civil unrest
=
Kingdom collapse

---

# Principle 3 — Systems Must Interconnect

No system should exist independently.

Example:

Technology affects:

- economy;
- warfare;
- culture;
- politics.

Culture affects:

- beliefs;
- laws;
- civilization direction.

Environment affects:

- resources;
- migration;
- survival.

---

# Principle 4 — Every Event Has A Cause

Nothing happens without influence.

Events originate from:

Previous Conditions

Current State

External Influence

Individual Choices

---

# Principle 5 — History Must Accumulate

The world remembers.

Past events influence:

- present conditions;
- future decisions;
- civilization identity.

---

# Principle 6 — Knowledge Is Progression

Project Draugr does not primarily measure advancement through levels.

Advancement comes through understanding.

Knowledge creates:

- capability;
- innovation;
- survival;
- influence.

---

# Principle 7 — Existence Is Finite But Impact Is Infinite

Individual beings end.

Their consequences continue.

# Design Principles

Every Project Draugr system must satisfy the following principles.

---

## Reality Over Convenience

The simulation should prioritize believable existence over simplified gameplay.

---

## Consistency Over Spectacle

A small consistent system is more valuable than a large inconsistent feature.

---

## Emergence Over Scripted Content

Systems should create possibilities.

---

## Consequence Over Reward

Actions should matter because they change reality.

---

## Knowledge Over Levels

Understanding should be the primary form of progression.

---

## Legacy Over Victory

The goal is not simply to win.

The goal is to leave something behind.

---

## Mystery Over Explanation

Not everything should be fully understood.

Unknown elements create depth.
