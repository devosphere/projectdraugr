# 21 – Multiplayer

> **Project Draugr**
>
> *You are not the protagonist. You are one life among many.*

---

# Purpose

The Multiplayer System allows multiple players to exist simultaneously within the same persistent world.

Every Chronicle occupies the same simulation.

There are no separate worlds, private instances, or isolated servers for gameplay.

Players naturally encounter one another through exploration.

---

# Design Philosophy

Project Draugr follows one immutable principle:

> **Other players are part of the world—not part of your story.**

No player is special.

No player receives priority.

The simulation remains impartial.

Every Chronicle follows the same physical laws.

---

# Shared Persistent World

All players inhabit one continuous world.

Examples:

If a player builds:

- a bridge
- a road
- a workshop
- a settlement

Every other player may eventually discover and use it.

Nothing exists only for its creator.

---

# Chronicle Independence

Every Chronicle is independent.

Chronicles possess their own:

- knowledge
- equipment
- memories
- discoveries
- relationships

Death affects only that Chronicle.

The world continues.

---

# Encounters

Meeting another Chronicle is not guaranteed.

Players naturally encounter one another through:

- exploration
- trade routes
- settlements
- shared resources
- conflict
- cooperation

Distance and geography determine encounters.

Not matchmaking.

---

# Communication

Communication occurs naturally.

Examples:

```text id="1zkn0e"
Talk to the traveler.

Wave.

Ask for directions.

Offer food.

Request help.
```

Conversation is a gameplay action.

It is not a separate chat application.

---

# Local Communication

Speech is local.

Only nearby Chronicles may hear it.

Distance, environment, and future mechanics may influence audibility.

Examples:

- shouting
- whispering
- speaking across a river

---

# Global Chat

The MVP intentionally excludes global chat.

There are no:

- world chat
- trade chat
- private messages
- guild chat

Communication requires physical presence.

---

# Cooperation

Players may cooperate naturally.

Examples:

- hunting
- construction
- farming
- exploration
- engineering
- settlement building

Cooperation emerges from player decisions.

It is never forced.

---

# Conflict

Conflict is a natural part of the world.

Players may choose to:

- steal
- attack
- defend
- negotiate
- flee

The simulation does not judge morality.

Every action produces consequences.

---

# Theft

Physical ownership is not mechanically protected.

If a Chronicle gains access to:

- containers
- workshops
- livestock
- supplies

they may attempt to steal them.

Future security systems may reduce accessibility through:

- locks
- keys
- guards
- walls
- traps

---

# Death

Chronicle death is permanent.

The deceased Chronicle:

- disappears as a living entity
- leaves behind a body
- leaves equipment
- leaves structures
- leaves history

Future players may discover the remains.

---

# Legacy

Every player contributes to the shared world's history.

Examples:

- settlements founded
- roads constructed
- technologies discovered
- civilizations established
- wars fought
- ruins abandoned

History belongs to everyone.

---

# Offline Players

When a player disconnects:

Their Chronicle no longer performs actions.

The body does not remain standing in the world.

Instead, the Chronicle safely leaves the simulation.

Upon returning, the Chronicle resumes from its previous safe state.

Future versions may support sleeping, inns, or campsites as preferred logout locations.

---

# Identity

Each Chronicle possesses:

- Chronicle ID
- Name
- Appearance (future)
- Equipment
- Reputation (future)

Identity emerges naturally through interaction.

---

# No Artificial Roles

Project Draugr does not assign:

- chosen ones
- heroes
- faction leaders
- kings

Leadership emerges socially.

Authority exists only because other players recognize it.

---

# Shared Discovery

Knowledge is personal.

Discovery is physical.

If one player discovers:

- a cave
- a river
- a ruin

Other players must still travel there themselves.

Knowledge may spread through:

- conversation
- journals
- maps
- books

Not through automatic synchronization.

---

# Future Expansion

The Multiplayer System may later support:

- trading
- contracts
- diplomacy
- governments
- towns
- kingdoms
- guilds
- legal systems
- economies
- religions

None of these exist by default.

They emerge through player interaction.

---

# Final Principle

Project Draugr is not a multiplayer game with isolated adventures.

It is one persistent world inhabited by many independent lives.

Every Chronicle is equal.

Every action leaves consequences.

Every player helps shape the history shared by everyone.
